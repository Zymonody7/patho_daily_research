## 摘要
背景：人诱导多能干细胞（hiPSCs）在重编程和培养过程中可能会产生基因组改变，这为临床应用带来了显著风险。目前的检测方法（如核型分析）通常无法识别关键的亚显微变异。这凸显了对全面基因组监测策略的迫切需求。方法：将三株人 iPSC 细胞系在体外连续培养 50 代，每 10 代评估一次基因组改变。评估方法包括用于检测染色体异常的核型分析、用于识别拷贝数变异（CNVs）和结构变异（SVs）的光学基因组图谱（OGM）、用于检测编码突变的全外显子组测序（WES）以及用于检测基因表达变化的 RNA 测序（RNA-seq）。结果：在长期培养过程中，我们在三株 hiPSC 细胞系中检测到了累积的染色体异常（如 12 号染色体三体）、SVs、CNVs 和序列突变。OGM 有效识别了低于核型分析分辨率的 SVs 和 CNVs，特别是复发性基因组异常，如 chr17q、chr12p 和 chr20q 的增加。WES 揭示了编码突变，包括生殖系短变异和新获得的体细胞突变，其中一些与肿瘤或疾病相关，如 CDH1、BCOR。转录水平的变化与基因组改变相关，包括癌基因（如 BCL2L1、KRAS 和 MDM2）的失调。结果表明，每种方法都有独特的检测能力和局限性，只有综合方法才能全面识别基因组异常。结论：本研究通过整合核型分析、OGM、WES 和 RNA-seq，建立了一套评估 hiPSCs 基因组改变的全面策略。该全面策略可应用于 hiPSC 克隆筛选、细胞库传代建立以及 hiPSC 衍生产品的质量控制等场景。它提供了一个可靠的遗传稳定性评估方案，为 hiPSC 相关产品的安全临床应用提供支持。

## Abstract
BACKGROUND: Human induced pluripotent stem cells (hiPSCs) may acquire genomic alterations during reprogramming and culture, which poses significant risks for clinical applications. Current detection methods, such as karyotyping analysis, often fail to identify critical submicroscopic variations. This highlights an urgent need for comprehensive genomic surveillance strategies. METHODS: Three human iPSC lines were continually cultured in vitro for 50 passages, with genome alterations evaluated every 10 passages. The evaluation methods included karyotyping to detect chromosomal abnormalities, optical genome mapping (OGM) to identify copy number variations (CNVs) and structural variants (SVs), whole-exome sequencing (WES) to detect coding mutations, and RNA sequencing (RNA-seq) to detect the changes of gene expression. RESULTS: We detected accumulating chromosomal abnormalities (e.g., trisomy 12), SVs, CNVs, and sequence mutations in three hiPSC lines during extended culture. OGM effectively identified SVs and CNVs below karyotyping resolution, particularly recurrent genome abnormalities such as gains on chr17q, chr12p and chr20q. WES revealed coding mutations, including germline short variants and newly acquired somatic mutations, some of which were associated with tumors or diseases, such as CDH1, BCOR. Transcriptional changes correlated with genomic alterations, including dysregulation of oncogenes such as BCL2L1, KRAS and MDM2. Results demonstrate that each method had unique detection capabilities and limitations, and only integrative approaches can comprehensively identify genomic abnormalities. CONCLUSIONS: This study established a comprehensive strategy for evaluating the genomic alterations of hiPSCs by integrating karyotyping, OGM, WES, and RNA-seq. This comprehensive strategy can be applied to scenarios such as hiPSC clone screening, establishment of cell bank passages, and quality control of hiPSC-derived products. It provides a reliable genetic stability evaluation protocol to support the safe clinical application of hiPSC-related products.
