## 摘要
青春双歧杆菌（Bifidobacterium adolescentis）是成年人肠道微生物群中最常见的双歧杆菌物种之一，流行率约为 60%。尽管其流行率很高，但对青春双歧杆菌的研究和表征尚不够深入，因此我们对其生理特征、遗传多样性以及与人类肠道微生物群其他成员或宿主之间潜在相互作用的理解仍是碎片化的。在本研究中，通过整合来自 131 个项目的公开数据和宏基因组组装结果，构建了一个包含 1,682 个青春双歧杆菌基因组的数据集，旨在揭示该物种独特的遗传特征。对青春双歧杆菌的泛基因组分析识别出 203 个在其他五种人类相关双歧杆菌中缺失的直系同源基因簇，其中 6 个经计算机预测编码该分类单元特有的功能。此外，预测有 2,597 个基因是通过水平基因转移获得的，包括编码参与宿主及其他微生物相互作用的细胞外结构的基因，以及针对噬菌体的防御机制。详细的系统发育分析显示，青春双歧杆菌物种内存在 7 个簇，每个簇都与菌株分离来源部分相关，表明地理来源塑造了系统发育分化。此外，对来自健康成年人的 10,000 多个肠道宏基因组进行的大规模分析显示，青春双歧杆菌与 36 种推测的有益共生菌和产丁酸分类群共存，突显了其作为参与成年人肠道微生物群网络构建的关键双歧杆菌物种的作用。重要性：为了全面探索微生物物种内的生物多样性，重建大量基因组至关重要。在本研究中，我们通过从人类肠道宏基因组样本中检索大量基因组，成功揭示了青春双歧杆菌的遗传多样性。青春双歧杆菌泛基因组的完整概览使我们能够研究区分该肠道共生菌与人类肠道微生物群中其他双歧杆菌物种的遗传特征。

## Abstract
UNLABELLED: Bifidobacterium adolescentis is one of the most frequently encountered bifidobacterial species present in the adult human gut microbiota, with a prevalence of approximately 60%. Despite its high prevalence, B. adolescentis has not been extensively studied and characterized, and our understanding of its physiological traits, genetic diversity, and potential interactions with other members of the human gut microbiota or with its host is therefore fragmentary. In the current study, a data set comprising 1,682 B. adolescentis genomes was compiled by combining publicly available data and metagenome assemblies from 131 projects to uncover the unique genetic characteristics of this species. A pangenome analysis of B. adolescentis identified 203 clusters of orthologous genes absent from the other five human-associated Bifidobacterium species, six of which were in silico predicted to encode functions unique to this taxon. Furthermore, 2,597 genes were predicted to have been acquired by horizontal gene transfer, including genes encoding extracellular structures involved in interaction with the host and other microorganisms, and phage defense mechanisms against bacteriophages. Detailed phylogenetic analysis revealed seven clusters within the B. adolescentis species, each partially associated with the origin of strain isolation, suggesting phylogenetic differentiation shaped by geographical strain origin. Moreover, a large-scale metagenomic analysis of over 10,000 human gut metagenomes from healthy adults revealed that B. adolescentis co-occurs with 36 putative beneficial commensals and butyrate-producing taxa, highlighting its role as a key bifidobacterial species involved in microbial networking within the adult human gut microbiota. IMPORTANCE: To comprehensively explore the biodiversity within a microbial species, the reconstruction of a substantial number of genomes is essential. In this study, we successfully uncovered the genetic diversity of Bifidobacterium adolescentis by retrieving a large number of genomes from human gut metagenomic samples. The complete overview of the B. adolescentis pangenome enabled us to investigate the genetic features that distinguish this gut commensal from other bifidobacterial species residing in the human intestinal microbiota.
