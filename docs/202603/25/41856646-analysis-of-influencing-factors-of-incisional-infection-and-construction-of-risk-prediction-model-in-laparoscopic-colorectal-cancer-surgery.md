## 摘要
目的：分析腹腔镜结直肠癌手术后切口手术部位感染（I-SSI）的危险因素，并构建风险预测模型，从而为临床精准预防提供依据。方法：本研究为回顾性观察研究，纳入2018年2月至2022年2月在江苏省扬州大学附属苏北人民医院接受腹腔镜结直肠癌根治术的919例患者。纳入标准：(1) 年龄≥18岁；(2) 术后病理证实为结肠癌或直肠癌；(3) 手术方式为腹腔镜根治术（包括腹腔镜辅助或全腹腔镜手术）；(4) 临床病理资料完整（包括基线数据、手术记录和术后随访记录）。排除标准：中转开腹或未完成预定手术方案；并发严重感染性疾病（如败血症或脓肿）或免疫缺陷疾病（如艾滋病或长期使用免疫抑制剂）；严重肝肾功能不全（Child-Pugh C级）；术前新辅助化疗；远处转移（IV期）；或并发其他恶性肿瘤。从电子病历系统中提取年龄、性别、体质指数（BMI）、病史、肿瘤大小、预防性抗生素使用、手术类型、手术部位、辅助切口方向和切口长度等数据。主要结局指标为I-SSI，根据2017年美国疾病控制与预防中心（CDC）手术部位感染预防指南进行诊断（标准包括脓性引流、病原体检出、感染体征如红肿热痛，或术后30天内由外科医生切开切口）。采用单因素和多因素Logistic回归分析筛选感染相关危险因素。利用R软件构建列线图（Nomogram）预测模型，并通过受试者工作特征（ROC）曲线和校准图评估其性能。结果：在919例患者中，55例（6.0%）发生I-SSI（感染组），其余864例（94.0%）为非感染组。与非感染组相比，感染组糖尿病和低蛋白血症的患病率显著更高，BMI更高，急诊手术和纵行切口的比例更高（均P<0.05）；术前预防性抗生素使用率较低（P=0.022），术前CT测量的肿瘤最小直径较大（P<0.001）。两组间切口长度与肿瘤最小直径的差值存在显著差异（P=0.011）。多因素分析显示，糖尿病（OR=2.05, 95%CI: 1.10-3.79, P=0.023）、低蛋白血症（OR=3.52, 95%CI: 1.73-7.17, P<0.001）、BMI（每增加1个单位：OR=1.10, 95%CI: 1.02-1.19, P=0.011）和纵行切口（OR=2.32, 95%CI: 1.09-4.92, P=0.029）是术后I-SSI的独立危险因素。相反，择期手术（OR=0.35, 95%CI: 0.16-0.80, P=0.012）、术前预防性抗生素使用（OR=0.44, 95%CI: 0.24-0.78, P=0.005）以及切口长度大于肿瘤最小直径（差值>1-2 cm：OR=0.35, 95%CI: 0.15-0.81, P=0.015；差值...）是独立保护因素。结论：糖尿病、低蛋白血症、BMI升高和纵行切口是腹腔镜结直肠癌手术I-SSI的独立危险因素，而择期手术、术前预防性抗生素使用以及确保切口长度大于肿瘤最小直径是独立保护因素。基于这些因素构建的列线图预测模型具有良好的预测性能，能有效识别术后切口感染的高危患者，为临床精准预防和干预提供参考。

## Abstract
Objective: To analyze the risk factors for incisional surgical site infection (I-SSI) after laparoscopic colorectal cancer surgery and to construct a risk prediction model, thereby providing a basis for precise clinical prevention. Methods: A retrospective observational study was conducted involving 919 patients who underwent laparoscopic radical colorectal cancer resection at Subei People's Hospital of Yangzhou University of Jiangsu Province from February 2018 to February 2022. The inclusion criteria were as follows: (1) Age ≥ 18 years; (2) Postoperative pathological confirmation of colon or rectal cancer; (3) Surgical approach involving laparoscopic radical resection (including laparoscopic-assisted or totally laparoscopic surgery); and (4) Complete clinicopathological data (including baseline data, surgical records, and postoperative follow-up records). The exclusion criteria included: conversion to open surgery or failure to complete the predetermined surgical plan; concurrent severe infectious diseases (e.g., sepsis or abscess) or immunodeficiency diseases (e.g., AIDS or long-term use of immunosuppressants); severe liver or kidney dysfunction (Child-Pugh grade C); preoperative neoadjuvant chemoradiotherapy; distant metastasis (Stage IV); or concurrent other malignancies. Data including age, gender, body mass index (BMI), medical history, tumor size, prophylactic antibiotic use, surgical type, surgical site, auxiliary incision direction, and incision length were extracted from the electronic medical record system. The primary outcome measure was I-SSI, diagnosed according to the 2017 Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC) guidelines for the prevention of surgical site infection (criteria included purulent drainage, pathogen detection, signs of infection such as pain/redness/swelling/heat, or incision opening by a surgeon within 30 days post-surgery). Univariate and multivariate Logistic regression analyses were used to screen for infection-related risk factors. A nomogram prediction model was constructed using R software, and its performance was evaluated using the Receiver Operating Characteristic (ROC) curve and calibration plot. Results: Among the 919 patients, 55 (6.0%) developed I-SSI (infection group), while the remaining 864 (94.0%) constituted the non-infection group. Compared with the non-infection group, the infection group had significantly higher prevalences of diabetes and hypoproteinemia, higher BMI, and higher proportions of emergency surgery and longitudinal incisions (all P<0.05); the rate of preoperative prophylactic antibiotic use was lower (P=0.022), and the minimum tumor diameter measured by preoperative CT was larger (P<0.001). The difference between incision length and minimum tumor diameter differed significantly between the two groups (P=0.011). Multivariate analysis indicated that diabetes (OR=2.05, 95%CI: 1.10-3.79, P=0.023), hypoproteinemia (OR=3.52, 95%CI: 1.73-7.17, P<0.001), BMI (per 1-unit increase: OR=1.10, 95%CI: 1.02-1.19, P=0.011), and longitudinal incision (OR=2.32, 95%CI: 1.09-4.92, P=0.029) were independent risk factors for postoperative I-SSI. Conversely, elective surgery (OR=0.35, 95%CI: 0.16-0.80, P=0.012), preoperative prophylactic antibiotic use (OR=0.44, 95%CI: 0.24-0.78, P=0.005), and an incision length greater than the minimum tumor diameter (Difference >1-2 cm: OR=0.35, 95%CI: 0.15-0.81, P=0.015; Difference Conclusions: Diabetes, hypoproteinemia, elevated BMI, and longitudinal incisions are independent risk factors for I-SSI in laparoscopic colorectal cancer surgery, whereas elective surgery, preoperative prophylactic antibiotic use, and ensuring the incision length is greater than the minimum tumor diameter are independent protective factors. The nomogram prediction model constructed based on these factors demonstrates good predictive performance and can effectively identify patients at high risk of postoperative incision infection, providing a reference for precise clinical prevention and intervention.
