## 摘要
大肠杆菌（E. Coli）、沙门氏菌和单核细胞增生李斯特菌等食源性病原体持续对全球公共卫生构成重大潜在威胁，因此，开发快速、准确且可现场部署的检测方法仍然是极其迫切的需求。本综述描述了用于检测这些重点病原体的高级生物传感平台的尖端案例，重点关注临床检测，并突出了电化学、光学和微流控传感模式。这得益于功能纳米材料、分子识别元件（包括适配体和纳米酶）以及表面工程策略的最新进展，这些进展使传感器在灵敏度、特异性以及对复杂食品基质的响应行为方面变得更加“智能”和完善。与此同时，将这些生物传感器与人工智能（AI）和机器学习（ML）相结合，实现了智能模式识别、实时分析和高速多重检测，从而将传统的检测系统转变为智能诊断设备。我们从生物传感器设计、信号转导机制、AI模型、性能验证以及在不同食品环境中的适用性等方面，对近期的案例研究进行了批判性评述。文中指出了主要挑战，包括基质干扰、生物识别元件的不稳定性、可扩展性的局限性以及监管标准化的需求。我们讨论了相关的技术缓解策略，包括比率传感、微流控预处理技术、可解释AI和可印刷电子技术。展望未来，我们讨论了自供电生物传感器、具有模块化病原体面板的生物传感器枢纽、区块链整合以及标准化验证流程。本综述通过将技术创新与实际应用相结合，为实现用于下一代食品安全监测的智能、稳健且符合监管要求的生物传感平台提供了前瞻性视角。图形摘要：[图片：见正文]

## Abstract
UNLABELLED: Foodborne pathogens such as Escherichia coli (E. Coli), Salmonella, and Listeria monocytogenes continue to pose a major potential threat to global public health and therefore rapid, accurate, and field-deployable detection methods are still extremely desirable. This review describes cutting-edge examples of advanced biosensing platforms for the strategy of detecting these priority pathogens, focusing on clinical detection and highlighting electrochemical, optical, and microfluidic sensing modalities. This has been enabled by recent advances in functional nanomaterials, molecular recognition elements (including aptamers and nanozymes), and surface engineering strategies rendering sensors much ‘smarter’/improved in terms of sensitivity, specificity, and behaviour towards complex food matrices. However blending these biosensors with artificial intelligence (AI) and Machine Learning (ML) enabled intelligent pattern recognition, real-time analytics, and multiplexing at high-speed, turning traditional detection systems into smart diagnostic devices. We critically review recent case studies in light of biosensor design, signal transduction mechanisms, models of AI, performance validation, and applicability in different food environments. The principal challenges are identified which include matrix interference, instability of biorecognition elements, limitations in scalability, and the need for regulatory standardization. We discuss these with associated mitigation strategies that are technically sound, including ratiometric sensing, microfluidic pre-treatment techniques, explainable AI, and printable electronics. Forward-looking, we discuss biosensors enabled by being self-powered, biosensor hubs with modular pathogen panels, blockchain incorporation, and standardized validation pipelines. This review offers a prospective view toward enabling intelligent, robust, and regulation-ready biosensing platforms for next-generation food safety monitoring through the bridging of technological innovations with practical implementation.
