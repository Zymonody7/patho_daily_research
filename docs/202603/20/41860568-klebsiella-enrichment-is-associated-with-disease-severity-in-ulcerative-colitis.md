## 摘要
背景与目的：溃疡性结肠炎（UC）是一种慢性炎症性肠病。本研究独特地对UC患者的粪便和黏膜微生物群及代谢物进行了平行的、按严重程度分层的比较。我们的目标是识别特定生态位（粪便 vs. 黏膜）和严重程度相关的微生物及代谢特征，阐明其潜在的临床应用价值。方法：一项前瞻性队列研究（ChiCTR2300071816）招募了来自南京医科大学第一附属医院和苏北人民医院（2022年1月至2024年12月）的83名UC患者（≥18岁）和30名健康对照者。收集了临床数据、粪便和直肠黏膜样本。进行了宏基因组测序和代谢组学分析。根据改良Mayo评分对疾病严重程度进行分层，以分析微生物群多样性、差异属、代谢物和富集的代谢途径。结果：UC患者的粪便微生物群α-多样性显著低于对照组（Shannon指数 4.15 vs. 5.44, p=0.005）；黏膜多样性无差异（p=0.63）。β-多样性没有差异。与轻中度UC相比，重度UC的α-多样性呈非显著性下降（粪便：3.99 vs. 4.37, p=0.14；黏膜：3.40 vs. 3.72, p=0.92），其粪便/黏膜中克雷伯氏菌属（Klebsiella）丰度显著升高，而丹毒丝菌属（Erysipelatoclostridium）和布劳特氏菌属（Blautia）丰度较低。代谢组学鉴定了363种粪便差异代谢物（如别嘌呤醇、组氨酸），富集在酪氨酸以及丙氨酸/天冬氨酸/谷氨酸代谢途径中。黏膜分析揭示了127种差异代谢物（如奎尼酸、鞘氨醇），涉及鞘脂代谢和赖氨酸合成。结论：UC表现出与严重程度相关的肠道菌群失调和代谢紊乱。克雷伯氏菌属丰度的升高提示其在疾病进展中具有致病作用。粪便和黏膜中不同的代谢途径改变为疾病分类和治疗靶点提供了新的见解。

## Abstract
BACKGROUND AND OBJECTIVE: Ulcerative colitis (UC), a chronic inflammatory bowel disease. This study uniquely undertook a parallel, severity-stratified comparison of both fecal and mucosal microbiota and metabolites in UC patients. Our objective was to identify niche-specific (fecal vs. mucosal) and severity-associated microbial and metabolic signatures, clarifying its potential clinical utility. METHODS: A prospective cohort study (ChiCTR2300071816) enrolled 83 UC patients (≥18 years) from First Affiliated Hospital of Nanjing Medical University and Northern Jiangsu People's Hospital (Jan 2022-Dec 2024) and 30 healthy controls. Clinical data, stool, and rectal mucosal samples were collected. Metagenomic sequencing and metabolomics were performed. Disease severity was stratified by modified Mayo score to analyze microbiota diversity, differential genera, metabolites, and enriched metabolic pathways. RESULTS: Fecal microbiota α-diversity was significantly lower in UC vs. controls (Shannon index 4.15 vs. 5.44, p=0.005); mucosal diversity showed no difference (p=0.63). Beta diversity did not differ. Severe UC exhibited a non-significant decrease in α-diversity (fecal: 3.99 vs. 4.37, p=0.14; mucosal: 3.40 vs. 3.72, p=0.92), significantly higher fecal/mucosal Klebsiella abundance, and lower Erysipelatoclostridium and Blautia abundance vs. mild-to-moderate UC. Metabolomics identified 363 fecal differential metabolites (e.g., allopurinol, histidine), enriching tyrosine and alanine/aspartate/glutamate metabolism pathways. Mucosal analysis revealed 127 differential metabolites (e.g., quinic acid, sphingosine), implicating sphingolipid metabolism and lysine synthesis. CONCLUSION: UC demonstrates gut dysbiosis and metabolic disruption correlating with severity. Elevated Klebsiella abundance suggests a pathogenic role in progression. Distinct fecal and mucosal metabolic pathway alterations provide novel insights for disease classification and therapeutic targeting.
