## 摘要
生态关联中的伙伴往往倾向于模仿其宿主的生物系统。我们假设微生物（特别是病原体）已经获得了表达与宿主 miPEPs 同源的短肽（pathoPEPs）的能力，从而调节相应宿主微小 RNA（miRNA）的表达以及 miRNA 靶基因的功能。本研究探讨了涉及甘蓝型油菜（Brassica napus）与其病原体芸苔根肿菌（Plasmodiophora brassicae）相互作用的病理系统。数据集包含接种了一种病原体分离株的两种植物基因型的 RNAseq 和核糖体图谱。通过计算机模拟分析，我们鉴定了由芸苔根肿菌产生的三个推定的 pathoPEPs 及其靶向的植物 miRNA 基因。研究发现，芸苔根肿菌对甘蓝型油菜的感染程度与 pathoPEPs 及其靶向 miRNA 基因的表达之间存在联系，且后两者的表达呈负相关。最后，我们鉴定了预测为 pathoPEP 调节的 miRNAs 靶基因的差异表达和翻译。这些基因涉及生长素途径、免疫防御、根系构型或碳水化合物代谢，被认为使芸苔根肿菌能够通过其 pathoPEPs 劫持植物的代谢途径（例如激素途径、糖合成、根系形态），从而促进其入侵。通过计算机模拟方法，本文首次描述了该宿主-病原体系统中 miPEPs 作为宿主转录后调节途径的参与情况。

## Abstract
The partners of an ecological association tend to copy the biological system of their hosts. We hypothesized that microorganisms, particularly pathogens, have acquired the ability to express short peptides (pathoPEPs) homologous to host miPEPs thus modulating the expression of the corresponding host microRNA (miRNA) and the function of miRNA-targeted genes. The pathosystem involving interactions between Brassica napus and its pathogen Plasmodiophora brassicae was studied. The dataset contains RNAseq and ribosomal profiling from 2 plant genotypes inoculated with one pathogen isolate. Using in silico analysis, we identified three putative pathoPEPs produced by P. brassicae and their targeted plant miRNA genes. A link between the level of infection of B. napus by P. brassicae and the expression of pathoPEPs and their targeted miRNA genes was found, with the expression of the latter two being inversely related. Finally, we identified differential expression and translation of genes predicted to be targets of pathoPEP-regulated miRNAs. These genes, involved in auxin pathway, immune defense, root architecture or carbohydrate metabolism, are thought to enable P. brassicae, through its pathoPEPs, to hijack plant's metabolic pathways (for example, hormonal pathways, sugar synthesis, root morphology), thereby facilitating its invasion. Using in silico approaches, the involvement of miPEPs from this host-pathogen system as a host post-transcriptional regulatory pathway is described herein for the first time.
