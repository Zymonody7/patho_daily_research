## 摘要
极度濒危的中华鲟（Acipenser sinensis）被养殖在控温循环水养殖系统（RAS）中，以提高其繁殖性能，从而增强野外种群。然而，人为的温度波动可能引发连锁的理化变化，从而破坏水体微生物组，进而增加机会性病原体的风险。鉴于 RAS 中的微生物群落在调节水质和宿主健康方面的关键作用，阐明其随温度变化的动态和相互作用网络对于减轻保护性水产养殖中的脆弱性至关重要。不同温度状态下的宏基因组分析揭示了温度驱动的微生物重组。在最佳产卵温度范围内，微生物组合表现出以不稳定的稀有生物圈为特征的两极分化多样性分布，鞘脂杆菌目（Sphingomonadales）和黄杆菌纲（Flavobacteriia）在细菌群落中占据主导地位。病原体的激增，包括不动杆菌属（Acinetobacter）、分枝杆菌属（Mycolicibacterium）和弧菌属（Vibrio），与这一最佳温度相吻合。相反，降温恢复了群落的同质性并抑制了病原体的扩张。氮循环途径分析确定 nirK 为一种热敏感生物标志物，尽管存在理化参数脱钩，但它在高温条件下仍能维持反硝化效率。RAS 中揭示了一种自我强化的理化连锁反应：溶解氧的升高依次触发了氨的耗尽、亚硝酸盐的积累和 pH 值的升高。硝化细菌属硝化螺旋菌属（Nitrospira）被确定为双功能核心，通过竞争性排斥抑制病原体，同时维持多个参数的平衡。我们的非侵入性生物监测框架有助于早期发现微生物失调和水质扰动。这种主动管理策略不仅增强了中华鲟的健康，还将微生物组管理定位为迁地保护的关键组成部分，为将濒危物种恢复与可持续水产养殖实践相结合提供了一个可扩展的模型。

## Abstract
The critically endangered Chinese sturgeon (Acipenser sinensis) is maintained in temperature-controlled recirculating aquaculture systems (RAS) to enhance reproductive performance for reinforcing wild population. However, anthropogenic thermal fluctuations can trigger cascading physicochemical alterations that may disrupt aquatic microbiomes, thereby elevating the risk of opportunistic pathogen. Given the pivotal role of microbial communities in RAS in regulating both water quality and host health, elucidating their temperature-dependent dynamics and interaction networks is critical for mitigating vulnerabilities in conservation aquaculture. Metagenomic profiling across thermal regimes revealed temperature-driven microbial restructuring. Within the optimal spawning temperature range, microbial assemblages exhibited bipolar diversity distributions characterized by destabilized rare biospheres, with Sphingomonadales and Flavobacteriia dominating the bacterial consortia. Pathogen surges, including Acinetobacter, Mycolicibacterium, and Vibrio, coincided with this thermal optimum. Conversely, temperature reduction restored community homogeneity and suppressed pathogen expansion. Analysis of nitrogen cycling pathways identified nirK as a thermally-sensitive biomarker that maintained denitrification efficiency under high-temperature conditions, despite decoupling of physicochemical parameters. A self-reinforcing physicochemical cascade was revealed in RAS: the elevation of dissolved oxygen sequentially triggers ammonium depletion, nitrite accumulation, and an increase in pH. The nitrifying genus Nitrospira was identified as a dual-functional linchpin, suppressing pathogens via competitive exclusion while maintaining equilibrium across multiple parameters. Our non-invasive biosurveillance framework facilitates early detection of microbial dysbiosis and water quality perturbations. This proactive management strategy not only enhances sturgeon health but also positions microbiome stewardship as a critical component of ex situ conservation, providing a scalable model for aligning endangered species recovery with sustainable aquaculture practices.
