## 摘要
历史采矿造成的遗留重金属污染重塑了沉积物微生物的组成和功能，直接影响污染物的归宿和生态系统健康。东大沟（中国白银）由于长期排放包括镉（Cd）、铜（Cu）、铅（Pb）和锌（Zn）在内的潜在有毒金属，具有显著的地球化学梯度。我们利用这一自然梯度来表征微生物分类和功能对金属胁迫的响应。对沿污染梯度的四个区域的沉积物样本进行了地球化学、金属浓度和微生物组成（细菌、古菌和真菌）分析，采用高通量扩增子测序，并利用 PICRUSt2 推断其功能潜力。研究发现，微生物群落结构和功能主要受金属浓度的影响，基于距离的冗余分析（db-RDA）分别解释了细菌、古菌和真菌 18.1%、12.4% 和 12.9% 的变异。镉被确定为细菌（r2 = 0.50, p = 0.001）和真菌（r2 = 0.38, p = 0.001）群落最强的单一预测因子。随着污染减轻，下游细菌多样性显著增加，Shannon 指数从源区（Source Zone）的 5.17 增加到远端区（Distal Zone）的 6.28（Tukey 多重比较检验，p < 0.05）。上游沉积物以耐金属类群为主，如 Sulfurifustis (17.4%) 和 Acidithiobacillus (5.0%)，而下游类群则转向具有多种代谢能力的异养属，如 Gallionella (4.8%)。尽管镉是关键预测因子，但古菌和真菌群落表现出比细菌更高的组成稳定性，其较低的 beta 离散度（ANOSIM R 分别为 0.3152 和 0.5762，而细菌为 0.7222）表明了潜在的功能冗余。宏基因组预测显示，污染区中金属解毒、厌氧呼吸和氧化应激响应相关基因显著富集。这些发现证实了微生物群落既是敏感的生物指示物，也是污染物动态的关键调节者，为利用微生物特征评估沉积物健康和监测修复效果提供了框架。

## Abstract
Legacy heavy metal pollution from historical mining restructures sediment microbial composition and function directly impacting contaminant fate and ecosystem health. The Dongdagou stream (Baiyin, China) possesses a pronounced geochemical gradient caused by long-term discharge of potentially toxic metals including Cd, Cu, Pb, and Zn. We employed this natural gradient to characterize microbial taxonomic and functional responses to metal stress. Sediment samples from four zones along the contamination gradient were analyzed for geochemistry, metal concentrations, and microbial composition (bacteria, archaea, and fungi) via high-throughput amplicon sequencing, with functional potential inferred using PICRUSt2. We found that microbial community structure and function were primarily shaped by metal concentration, with db-RDA explaining 18.1 %, 12.4 %, and 12.9 % of the variance for bacteria, archaea, and fungi, respectively. Cadmium was identified as the strongest individual predictor for both bacterial (r2 = 0.50, p = 0.001) and fungal (r2 = 0.38, p = 0.001) communities. Bacterial diversity increased significantly downstream as contamination declined, with Shannon diversity increasing from 5.17 in the Source Zone to 6.28 in the Distal Zone (Tukey's multiple comparison test, p < 0.05). Upstream sediments were dominated by metal-tolerant taxa such as Sulfurifustis (17.4 %) and Acidithiobacillus (5.0 %), while downstream taxa shifted to heterotrophic genera like Gallionella (4.8 %) with diverse metabolic capabilities. Despite cadmium being a key predictor, archaeal and fungal communities demonstrated greater compositional stability than bacteria, as shown by their lower beta-dispersion (ANOSIM R = 0.3152 and 0.5762, respectively, compared to 0.7222 for bacteria), indicating potential functional redundancy. Metagenomic predictions revealed a significant enrichment of genes for metal detoxification, anaerobic respiration, and oxidative stress response in polluted zones. These findings establish that microbial communities are both sensitive bioindicators and key mediators of contaminant dynamics, providing a framework for using microbial signatures to assess sediment health and monitor remediation efficacy.
