## 摘要
背景：结核病（TB）是全球最致命的细菌性传染病之一，多耐药（MDR）和广泛耐药（XDR）菌株的病例呈上升趋势。含贝达喹啉（BDQ）的方案已成为治疗 MDR/XDR-TB 的重要手段，而对 BDQ 的耐药性也随之增加。了解基因突变对于早期检测 BDQ 耐药菌株并维持这些药物的有效性至关重要。本研究旨在综述全球范围内与 BDQ 耐药结核病相关的突变。方法：本研究在 PubMed、Scopus、Web of Science 和 Embase 数据库中系统检索了 2014 年至 2025 年间全球报告 BDQ 耐药结核病及其相关基因的研究，关键词包括 TB、XDR、MDR、BDQ 和基因。结果：本系统综述纳入了来自全球的 40 项研究和 25,234 份 MDR 和 XDR-TB 患者样本。结果显示，世界卫生组织（WHO）各区域的 BDQ 耐药性存在显著差异，东地中海地区最高，西太平洋地区最低。此外，收集的数据显示，在所研究的大洲中，非洲的耐药性最高，美洲最低。国家分布显示，伊朗（n = 24）、摩尔多瓦（n = 26）和亚美尼亚（n = 35）的耐药率高于分析中的其他国家，而意大利（n = 1001）和菲律宾（n = 724）的耐药率较低。在遗传学方面，耐药相关突变主要分别观察于 Rv0678、atpE 和 pepQ 基因。结论：鉴于 BDQ 耐药性的增加和区域差异，建立早期检测系统、基因组监测、强有力的药物政策执行和快速诊断对于维持治疗效果并遏制耐药性传播至关重要。未来的研究应侧重于阐明耐药机制并开发新型治疗策略。

## Abstract
BACKGROUND: Tuberculosis (TB) is one of the deadliest bacterial infectious diseases worldwide, with rising cases of multidrug-resistant (MDR) and extensively drug-resistant (XDR) strains. Bedaquiline (BDQ)-containing regimens have become important for the treatment of MDR/XDR-TB, and resistance to BDQ is increasing. Understanding genetic mutations is crucial for early detection of BDQ-resistant strains and thus maintaining the effectiveness of these drugs. This study aimed to review mutations associated with BDQ-resistant TB globally. METHODS: This study systematically searched the keywords TB, XDR, MDR, BDQ, and genes in the PubMed, Scopus, Web of Science, and Embase databases for studies reporting BDQ-resistant TB and their associated genes globally from 2014 to 2025. RESULTS: This systematic review included 40 studies and 25,234 patient samples with MDR and XDR-TB from around the world. Results showed significant variation in BDQ resistance across the World Health Organization (WHO) regions, with the highest in the Eastern Mediterranean and the lowest in the Western Pacific. Furthermore, the data collected showed that, among the continents studied, resistance was highest in Africa and lowest in the Americas. The country distribution showed that resistance rates were higher in Iran (n = 24), Moldova (n = 26), and Armenia (n = 35), and lower in Italy (n = 1001) and the Philippines (n = 724) than in other countries in the analysis. Genetically, the most resistance-associated mutations were observed in the Rv0678, atpE, and pepQ genes, respectively. CONCLUSION: Given the increasing BDQ resistance and regional variability, it is essential to develop early detection systems, genomic surveillance, robust drug policy enforcement, and rapid diagnostics to maintain treatment effectiveness and curb the spread of resistance. Future research should focus on elucidating resistance mechanisms and developing novel therapeutic strategies.
