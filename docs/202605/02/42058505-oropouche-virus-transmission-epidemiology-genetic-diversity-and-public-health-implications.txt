## Abstract

Historically endemic to parts of South America, Oropouche virus (OROV) has caused an estimated 500,000 infections since its discovery, with a marked geographic expansion beyond the Amazon basin into other regions of South America and the Caribbean since late 2023. This Review synthesises current evidence on OROV epidemiology, transmission dynamics, clinical manifestations, diagnostics, viral diversity, and public health impact, with the primary objective of identifying critical knowledge gaps and outlining priorities for surveillance, research, and control. Human transmission occurs primarily via _Culicoides paraensis_ midges, while the competence of other vectors, the role of animal reservoirs in sustaining sylvatic transmission, and the contribution of vertical and sexual transmission remain incompletely understood. Although most infections are self-limiting, reports of neurological disease, Guillain-Barré syndrome, adverse pregnancy outcomes, and rare fatalities highlight uncertainties regarding pathogenicity, risk factors for severe disease, and long-term sequelae. The known teratogenicity of related Simbu serogroup orthobunyaviruses in animals further raises concerns about foetal risk in humans. Environmental change, expanding vector ranges, and viral evolution are likely contributing to outbreak emergence and geographic spread. Based on the available evidence, this review highlights priority gaps in epidemiological surveillance, diagnostics and genomic monitoring, vector competence and ecology, transmission pathways, and countermeasure development. Addressing these gaps through coordinated surveillance, improved laboratory capacity, targeted vector control, and focused research will be essential to mitigate the public health impact of OROV and reduce the risk of further spread.

**Keywords:** Emerging arbovirus; Neurotropic virus; Oropouche fever; Oropouche virus; Orthobunyavirus; Simbu serogroup.

**Fig. 4**
**Maximum Likelihood phylogenetic tree inferred from the M segment of OROV sequences (n** = **907).** The tips are coloured by major OROV clades. The OROV BR-2015–2024 clade was collapsed to improve visualisation and is shown by a green triangle. The tree is mid-point rooted. In brief, all available Oropouche virus nucleotide sequences were retrieved from NCBI GenBank on 28 Oct 2025 using the query “oropouche [All Fields]”. Non-Oropouche or misannotated accessions were removed, after which the sequences were separated by genome segment (n = 3103). Isolates with sequences for all three OROV segments (i.e. S, M and L) were retained for downstream analysis (n = 994), and then focused on the M segment and only retained sequences ≥80% of the canonical M length (≥3410 nt, relative to NC_005775.1). Where multiple entries existed for the same isolate, the single sequence with length closest to the canonical length (4263 nt) was kept. The M-segment sequences were aligned with MAFFT (v7.526) and thereafter trimmed the alignment manually to the start and end of the M gene. Maximum-likelihood phylogeny was inferred with IQ-TREE (v3.0.1) using the ModelFinder option to select the best-fit nucleotide substitution model. The inferred tree was visualised using FigTree (v1.4.4).
