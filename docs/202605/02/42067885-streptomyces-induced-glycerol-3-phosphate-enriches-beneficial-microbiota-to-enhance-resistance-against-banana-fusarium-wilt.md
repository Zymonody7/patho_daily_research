## 摘要
背景：尖孢镰刀菌古巴专化型热带4号生理小种（Foc TR4）是香蕉枯萎病的病原体，这是一种破坏性的土传病害。利用链霉菌等拮抗微生物是防治真菌病害的一种极具前景的策略。然而，由于对微生物-植物-病原体相互作用的理解不完整，其田间应用受到限制。结果：本研究表明，海洋来源的马来西亚链霉菌（Streptomyces malaysiensis）WHL7在体外对多种植物致病真菌（尤其是Foc TR4）表现出强烈的拮抗活性。在自然土壤的盆栽实验中，WHL7处理显著将枯萎病的发病率从接种Foc TR4对照组的55%降低至15%。在灭菌土壤中未观察到保护效果，表明其生物防治效力依赖于本土微生物群落。综合代谢组学分析显示，WHL7刺激了关键根系分泌物3-磷酸甘油（G3P）的分泌。该化合物富集了根际中的有益芽孢杆菌（Bacillus）和假单胞菌（Pseudomonas）物种。与Foc TR4处理组相比，外源施用G3P使Foc TR4丰度降低了73%，抑制了病原体感染，并持续增加了芽孢杆菌和假单胞菌的种群数量。这些富集的微生物通过诱导植物的系统获得性抗性，与疾病严重程度的降低直接相关。结论：虽然马来西亚链霉菌WHL7在体外通过拮抗化合物直接抑制Foc TR4，但在盆栽实验中，它通过重塑根际微生物组间接控制疾病。这种重塑通过G3P介导的信号通路诱导宿主抗性。本研究强调了G3P介导的启动效应是管理土传病害的一种可持续策略。视频摘要。

## Abstract
BACKGROUND: Fusarium oxysporum f. sp. cubense Tropical Race 4 (Foc TR4) is the causal agent of banana Fusarium wilt, a destructive soil-borne disease. Using antagonistic microorganisms, such as Streptomyces species, offers a promising strategy for controlling fungal diseases. However, their field application is limited by an incomplete understanding of microbe-plant-pathogen interactions. RESULTS: This study shows that the marine-derived Streptomyces malaysiensis WHL7 exhibits strong antagonistic activity against multiple phytopathogenic fungi in vitro, particularly Foc TR4. In pot experiments using natural soil, WHL7 treatment significantly reduced the incidence of Fusarium wilt from 55% in the Foc TR4-inoculated control to 15%. No protective effect was observed in sterilized soil, indicating that its biocontrol efficacy depends on the indigenous microbial community. Integrated metabolomic analysis reveals that WHL7 stimulates the key root secretion of glycerol-3-phosphate (G3P). The compound enriches beneficial Bacillus and Pseudomonas species in the rhizosphere. Compared to the Foc TR4-treated group, exogenous application of G3P reduced Foc TR4 abundance by 73%, inhibited pathogenic infection, and consistently increased the populations of Bacillus and Pseudomonas. These enriched microbes were directly linked with reduced disease severity by inducing systemic ACQUIRED RESISTANCE OF plant. CONCLUSIONS: While S. malaysiensis WHL7 directly suppresses Foc TR4 via antagonistic compounds in vitro, it controls disease in pot experiments indirectly by reshaping the rhizosphere microbiome. This restructuring induces host resistance through a g3p-mediated signaling pathway. the study highlights g3p-mediated priming as a sustainable strategy for managing soil-borne diseases.
