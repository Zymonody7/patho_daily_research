## 摘要
背景：肠道微生物群通过影响循环代谢物及随后的基因调节网络来适应并塑造宿主的代谢状态，从而通过肠-肝轴等连接对多种器官产生系统性影响。饮食、年龄和宿主遗传等众多变量调节肠道微生物组的组成，但它们之间的相互作用以及与宿主分子表型的特定关联和机制联系尚未得到完全注释。在遗传多样化的群体中采用综合多组学方法，为剖析这些相互作用并识别宿主表型（如体重以及与肠道和肝脏基因表达途径的分子关联）的预测性微生物特征提供了机会。结果：我们对来自 175 个不同队列的 232 只小鼠的盲肠宏基因组、宏转录组和宿主转录组进行了测序、比对和整合，这些小鼠根据低脂常规饮食 (CD) 或高脂饮食 (HF)、四个成年年龄段（约 180 至 730 天）以及 43 种不同基因型（近交 BXD 系）进行分类。遗传和饮食对微生物群丰度和活性的影响最强，其次是年龄。高脂饮食显著降低了所有年龄和所有基因型的多样性，改变了 300 多种物种。基于微生物谱的机器学习模型能够可靠地预测饮食组内的体重（CD 的 AUC = 0.84，HF 的 AUC = 0.79）和实足年龄（AUC = 0.84），当将顶级微生物特征与肝脏蛋白质组学整合时，年龄预测的模型性能提升至 0.95。表达数据的网络分析揭示了基因、通路与特定微生物之间的联系，包括盲肠 Ido1 表达与产生短链脂肪酸 (SCFA) 的毛螺菌科 (Lachnospiraceae) 之间的负相关，表明饮食脂肪可能通过微生物群的变化来调节宿主的色氨酸代谢。结论：与早期的短读长 16S 分析相比，全宏基因组和宏转录组测序方法极大地扩展了微生物组分析的图谱。所得数据集对数百种可唯一识别的微生物进行了定量，这些微生物可用于创建一套针对衰老和肥胖的高预测性微生物生物标志物。在受控小鼠群体中进行训练时，这些结果表明，即使考虑到遗传和饮食变异，微生物组分析对年龄和体重（AUC = 0.84）等复杂读数也能实现高预测能力（多组学整合后 AUC = 0.95），从而为生物标志物的开发建立了框架。虽然目前许多细菌在物种水平上的

## Abstract
BACKGROUND: The gut microbiota adapts to and shapes the host's metabolic state through affecting circulating metabolites and consequent gene regulatory networks, resulting in systemic influences in diverse organs via connections such as the gut-liver axis. Numerous variables such as diet, age, and host genetics modulate the composition of the gut microbiome, but their interactions and specific associative and mechanistic links to host molecular phenotypes remain incompletely unannotated. Integrated multi-omics approaches in genetically diverse populations offer an opportunity to dissect these interactions and identify predictive microbial signatures for host phenotypes, such as body weight and molecular associations with gene expression pathways in gut and liver. RESULTS: We sequenced, aligned, and integrated the cecal metagenome, metatranscriptome, and host transcriptome from 232 mice across 175 distinct cohorts according to a low-fat chow diet (CD) or a high-fat diet (HF), four adult ages (between roughly 180 to 730 days of age), and 43 distinct genotypes (inbred BXD strains). Genetics and diet exerted the strongest influence on microbiota abundance and activity, followed by age. HF feeding significantly reduced diversity across all ages and all genotypes, altering > 300 species. Machine learning models based on microbial profiles reliably predicted body weight within dietary group (AUC = 0.84 for CD, 0.79 for HF) and chronological age (AUC = 0.84), with model performance of age prediction rising to 0.95 when integrating top microbial features with liver proteomics. Network analyses of expression data revealed links between genes, pathways, and specific microbes, including a negative association between cecal Ido1 expression and short-chain fatty acid (SCFA)-producing Lachnospiraceae, suggesting dietary fat may modulate host tryptophan metabolism through microbiota shifts. CONCLUSIONS: Whole metagenome and metatranscriptome sequencing approaches have massively expanded the landscape of microbiome analysis compared to earlier short-read 16S analyses. The resulting datasets quantify hundreds of uniquely identifiable microbes, which can be used to create sets of highly predictive microbial biomarkers for aging and obesity. When trained on controlled mouse populations, these results demonstrate that microbiome profiling can achieve high predictive capacity (AUC = 0.95 with multi-omics integration) for complex readouts such as age and body weight (AUC = 0.84), even considering genetic and dietary variation, establishing a framework for biomarker development. While at present many bacteria are still functionally unannotated at the species level, multi-omics approaches - including gene expression from the host tissues - provide insights into the functional associations of specific taxa in the microbiome.
