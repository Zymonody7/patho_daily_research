## 摘要
未标注：侵袭性真菌感染与高死亡率相关，且由于抗真菌药物储备有限以及耐药性的迅速出现，治疗变得日益困难。迫切需要结合强效抗真菌活性、低宿主毒性、体内稳定性和较低耐药产生倾向的新型治疗策略。抗菌肽（AMPs）作为对抗抗菌素耐药性的一类极具前景的化合物脱颖而出。利用 AMPs 的独特特性，我们此前开发了一种合成具有强大杀菌活性的随机多肽混合物（RPMs）的新方法，可对抗耐药细菌。在此，我们评估了 RPMs 的抗真菌潜力，并证明了 FK20（L-苯丙氨酸-L-赖氨酸，20 聚体）对主要人类真菌病原体（包括念珠菌属、新型隐球菌和烟曲霉）具有物种依赖性的广谱活性，特别是对多重耐药病原体耳念珠菌（Candida auris）具有极高的效力。机制分析显示，其伴随细胞内渗透的快速膜和细胞壁破坏，与膜活性抗真菌活性一致。重要的是，实验进化分析表明，耳念珠菌产生耐药性的能力显著降低。FK20 抑制了生物膜的形成，并对成熟的、预先形成的生物膜表现出显著活性，无论是单独使用还是与卡泊芬净联合使用均具有协同作用。最后，FK20 在全身性念珠菌病的小鼠模型中显示出显著的治疗效果。总的来说，这些发现确立了 RPMs 作为一个具有广谱活性、生物膜效力和低耐药特征的多功能抗真菌平台，突显了其作为对抗耐药真菌感染的新型治疗策略的前景。重要性：侵袭性真菌感染的患病率上升，特别是在免疫功能受损的人群中，已成为一个关键的公共卫生问题。然而，抗真菌药物的开发未能跟上这一日益增长的需求，治疗选择仍局限于少数几类药物。多重耐药真菌病原体（如耳念珠菌）的出现通过降低现有疗法的疗效并增加治疗失败的风险，进一步加剧了这一危机。在本研究中，我们评估了由 L-苯丙氨酸和 L-赖氨酸组成的随机多肽混合物 FK20 的抗真菌潜力。FK20 对耳念珠菌和其他临床相关的念珠菌属表现出强效活性，损害生物膜形成，并与卡泊芬净表现出协同作用。重要的是，FK20 限制了耐药性的产生，并在全身性念珠菌病的小鼠模型中证明了治疗效果。这些发现确立了 RPMs 作为一类具有广谱活性和临床潜力的新型抗真菌药物，可用于对抗耐药真菌感染。

## Abstract
UNLABELLED: Invasive fungal infections are associated with high mortality and are increasingly difficult to treat due to a limited antifungal arsenal and the rapid emergence of drug resistance. Novel therapeutic strategies that combine potent antifungal activity, low host toxicity, in vivo stability, and a reduced propensity for resistance development are urgently needed. Antimicrobial peptides (AMPs) stand out as a promising class of compounds to combat antimicrobial resistance. Leveraging the unique properties of AMPs, we previously developed a novel approach to synthesize random peptide mixtures (RPMs) with robust bactericidal activity against drug-resistant bacteria. Here, we evaluate the antifungal potential of RPMs and demonstrate species-dependent, broad-spectrum activity of FK20 (L-phenylalanine-L-lysine, 20-mer) against major human fungal pathogens, including Candida spp., Cryptococcus neoformans, and Aspergillus fumigatus, with particularly high potency against the multidrug-resistant pathogen Candida auris. Mechanistic analyses revealed rapid membrane and cell wall disruption accompanied by intracellular penetration, consistent with membrane-active antifungal activity. Importantly, experimental evolution assays demonstrated a markedly reduced capacity for resistance development in C. auris. FK20 inhibited biofilm formation and displayed substantial activity against mature, pre-formed biofilms, both alone and synergistically in combination with caspofungin. Finally, FK20 showed significant therapeutic efficacy in a murine model of systemic candidiasis. Collectively, these findings establish RPMs as a versatile antifungal platform with broad-spectrum activity, biofilm efficacy, and a low resistance footprint, highlighting their promise as a novel therapeutic strategy against drug-resistant fungal infections. IMPORTANCE: The rising prevalence of invasive fungal infections, particularly among immunocompromised individuals, has become a critical public health concern. However, antifungal drug development has not kept pace with this growing need, and treatment options remain limited to a small number of drug classes. The emergence of multidrug-resistant fungal pathogens, such as Candida auris, further exacerbates this crisis by reducing the efficacy of existing therapeutics and increasing the risk of treatment failure. In this study, we evaluate the antifungal potential of FK20, a random peptide mixture (RPM) composed of L-phenylalanine and L-lysine. FK20 displays potent activity against C. auris and other clinically relevant Candida species, impairs biofilm formation, and exhibits synergy with caspofungin. Importantly, FK20 limits the emergence of resistance and demonstrates therapeutic efficacy in a murine model of systemic candidiasis. These findings establish RPMs as a promising new class of antifungals with broad-spectrum activity and clinical potential against drug-resistant fungal infections.
