## 摘要
登革病毒（DENV）、基孔肯雅病毒（CHIKV）和寨卡病毒（ZIKV）等虫媒病毒由伊蚊传播，主要在热带和亚热带地区流行。随着全球变暖，其地理分布范围不断扩大，对公共卫生的威胁日益增加。中国云南省与东南亚接壤，由于频繁的跨境人员流动，是病毒输入的重点地区。然而，目前对急性发热患者中这些虫媒病毒的系统监测仍显不足。本研究对2017年至2023年间中缅边境990名急性发热患者的血清样本进行了宏基因组测序。通过PCR和病毒分离对病原体进行了确认，并利用系统发育和时空分析推断了病毒的起源及传播动态。研究确认了2019年的一次CHIKV暴发，其毒株与缅甸和泰国的毒株密切相关。识别出DENV的全部四种血清型（1-4型），且每年的优势血清型各异。检测到的ZIKV与缅甸毒株高度同源。此外，研究还发现了合并感染病例，包括CHIKV分别与DENV-1、DENV-3、ZIKV的合并感染，以及DENV-1与DENV-2的合并感染。对CHIKV的贝叶斯时空分析重建了其全球传播路径，表明2019年中国的暴发可能起源于印度，并先后经由孟加拉国、泰国和缅甸传播。此外，研究还检测到了肠道病毒、肝炎病毒、Saffold病毒和鼻病毒。本研究揭示了全面的病原体谱，包括DENV、CHIKV和ZIKV的共同流行，强调了虫媒病毒输入中国的潜在风险，并突显了加强边境监测的必要性。重要性：登革病毒（DENV）、基孔肯雅病毒（CHIKV）和寨卡病毒（ZIKV）等虫媒病毒的分布范围正在扩大，威胁着全球公共卫生。云南地处中国与东南亚交界地带，极易受到病毒输入的影响。通过对急性发热患者进行病毒宏基因组测序，本研究揭示了全面的病原体谱以及DENV、CHIKV和ZIKV的共同流行情况。系统发育分析显示，这些虫媒病毒与缅甸和泰国的毒株密切相关，表明跨境病毒输入可能十分频繁。同时，通过贝叶斯时空分析重建了CHIKV的全球传播路径，为区域性虫媒病毒防控提供了重要见解。这些发现表明云南是病毒输入的关键界面，并强调了加强边境监测和预警系统以减缓虫媒病毒传播的紧迫性。

## Abstract
UNLABELLED: Arboviruses such as dengue virus (DENV), chikungunya virus (CHIKV), and Zika virus (ZIKV) are transmitted by Aedes mosquitoes and mainly circulate in tropical and subtropical regions. With global warming, their geographic range is expanding, increasing their threat to public health. Yunnan Province, China, bordering Southeast Asia, is a hotspot for viral importation due to intensive cross-border mobility. However, systematic surveillance for these arboviruses among acute febrile patients remains insufficient. We performed metagenomic sequencing on serum specimens from 990 acute febrile patients at the China-Myanmar border between 2017 and 2023. The pathogens were confirmed by PCR and viral isolation. Phylogenetic and spatiotemporal analyses were used to infer viral origins and transmission dynamics. In this study, a CHIKV outbreak was confirmed in 2019, with strains closely related to those from Myanmar and Thailand. Four DENV serotypes 1-4 were identified, with the predominant serotype varying annually. ZIKV was detected and closely related to strains from Myanmar. Co-infections were identified, including one case each of CHIKV with DENV-1, CHIKV with DENV-3, CHIKV with ZIKV, and DENV-1 with DENV-2. Bayesian spatiotemporal analysis of CHIKV reconstructed global transmission routes, indicating that the 2019 outbreak in China likely originated in India and spread sequentially through Bangladesh, Thailand, and Myanmar. In addition, we also detected enterovirus, hepatitis virus, Saffold virus, and rhinovirus. This study reveals a comprehensive spectrum of pathogens, including the co-circulation of DENV, CHIKV, and ZIKV, and underscores the potential risk of arbovirus importation into China, highlighting the need for strengthened border surveillance. IMPORTANCE: Arboviruses, including dengue virus (DENV), chikungunya virus (CHIKV), and Zika virus (ZIKV), are expanding their range and threatening global public health. Yunnan, situated along the China-Southeast Asia border, is highly susceptible to viral introduction. By applying viral metagenomic sequencing to acute febrile patients, this study uncovered a comprehensive spectrum of pathogens and the co-circulation of DENV, CHIKV, and ZIKV. Phylogenetic analyses revealed that arboviruses were closely related to strains from Myanmar and Thailand, indicating possible frequent cross-border viral introductions. Meanwhile, we reconstructed the global transmission pathways of CHIKV through Bayesian spatiotemporal analysis, providing valuable insights for regional prevention and control of arboviruses. These findings demonstrate that Yunnan serves as a critical interface for viral importation and underscore the urgent need to strengthen border surveillance and early warning systems to mitigate the spread of arboviruses.
