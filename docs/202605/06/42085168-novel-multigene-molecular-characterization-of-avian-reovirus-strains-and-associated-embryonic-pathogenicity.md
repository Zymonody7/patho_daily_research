## 摘要
禽呼肠孤病毒（ARV）感染会导致家禽出现多种健康问题，包括腱鞘炎/关节炎、肝炎、吸收不良等，从而造成巨大的经济损失。因此，定期监测 ARV 毒株对于检测新变异株以减轻临床疾病至关重要。传统的分类方法主要集中在单个基因 σC 上，该基因编码诱导中和抗体的病毒刺突蛋白。然而，其他衣壳蛋白也可能通过诱导宿主抗病毒免疫反应发挥作用。在本研究中，分别分析了由 L3、M2、S1 和 S3 基因组片段编码的 λC、μB、σC 和 σB 蛋白，以根据胚胎致病性和基因型-表型关系对临床 ARV 毒株进行分类。胚胎接种实验揭示了毒株依赖性的致病性，范围从无肌腱病变（毒株 23-272）到伴有纤维蛋白异嗜性结痂的多灶性溃疡（22-806）以及多灶性坏死性肝炎（23-087）。两两对数秩检验和 Cox 回归分析表明，存活率存在显著的毒株依赖性差异，定义了从快速胚胎致死到延迟死亡的梯度，其中一株低毒力毒株表现出部分存活。虽然 λB-σB 双基因分型方法与毒力集群的一致性不显著，但基于二级结构的 μB-λC-σC 三重模型成为更好的毒力预测指标。值得注意的是，σC 在抗原表位上表现出显著的变异性，而 σB 则相对保守。这些结果可能解释了疫苗对近期变异株效力下降的原因，并支持将疫苗设计从 σC 转向 σB 抗原或两者的组合，以实现更广泛的保护。总之，这种基于结构的多基因框架增强了 ARV 分类，将基因型与毒力联系起来，并为改进疫苗接种策略提供了信息。重要性：禽呼肠孤病毒（ARV）对家禽健康和生产构成重大威胁，这主要是由于其与腱鞘炎/关节炎的关联，以及高遗传多样性驱动的疫苗耐药毒株的出现。有效的防控需要一种超越仅基于 σC 蛋白的传统方案的 ARV 分类系统，事实证明传统方案不足以捕捉致病多样性。我们提出了一种新型的、基于结构的三重基因分型方法，结合了主要衣壳蛋白（μB）、转塔蛋白（λC）和病毒附着蛋白（σC）。该方法提高了对鸡胚疾病严重程度的预测能力，同时减少了对大规模动物攻毒模型的依赖。此外，我们的蛋白质分析强调，保守的 σB 蛋白或 σC 与 σB 蛋白的组合可能是具有前景的疫苗靶点，能够针对多种 ARV 毒株提供更广泛的保护，从而为改进毒株分类和合理的疫苗设计提供新途径。

## Abstract
UNLABELLED: Avian reovirus (ARV) infection causes a variety of health problems in poultry, including tenosynovitis/arthritis, hepatitis, malabsorption, etc., leading to huge economic losses. Therefore, regular monitoring of ARV strains is crucial for detecting new variants to mitigate clinical disease. Traditional classification approaches have focused primarily on a single gene, σC, which encodes the viral spike protein that elicits neutralizing antibodies. However, additional capsid proteins could play a role by inducing a host antiviral immune response. In this study, the λC, μB, σC, and σB proteins encoded by the L3, M2, S1, and S3 genomic segments, respectively, were analyzed to classify clinical ARV strains with respect to embryonic pathogenicity and genotype-phenotype relationships. Embryo inoculation revealed strain-dependent pathogenicity ranging from the absence of tendon lesions (strain 23-272) to multifocal ulceration with fibrinoheterophilic crusting (22-806) and multifocal necrotizing hepatitis (23-087). Pairwise log-rank and Cox regression tests demonstrated highly significant strain-dependent differences in survival, defining a gradient from rapid embryonic lethality to delayed mortality, with one low-virulence strain exhibiting partial survival. While the λB-σB dual genotyping approach showed insignificant concordance with virulence clusters, a secondary structure-based μB-λC-σC triple model emerged as a better predictor of virulence. Notably, σC exhibited substantial variability in antigenic epitopes, whereas σB remained relatively conserved. These results might explain reduced vaccine efficacy against recent variants and support shifting vaccine design from σC to σB antigen or a combination to achieve broader protection. In conclusion, this multigene, structure-informed framework enhances ARV classification, links genotypes with virulence, and informs improved vaccination strategies. IMPORTANCE: Avian reovirus (ARV) represents a major threat to poultry health and production, primarily through its association with tenosynovitis/arthritis and the emergence of vaccine-resistant strains driven by high genetic diversity. Effective control requires an ARV classification system beyond traditional schemes based solely on the σC protein, which have proven insufficient to capture pathogenic diversity. We propose a novel, structure-informed triple genotyping approach incorporating the major capsid (μB), the turret (λC), and the virus attachment (σC) proteins. This method improves the prediction of disease severity in chicken embryos while reducing reliance on extensive animal challenge models. Furthermore, our protein-based analysis highlights that the conserved σB protein or a combination of σC & σB protein might be a promising vaccine target capable of providing broader protection across diverse ARV strains, thereby offering new avenues for both improved strain classification and rational vaccine design.
