## Abstract

**Objectives:** Garden sage (Salvia officinalis L.) is a traditional medicinal plant known for its rich bioactive secondary metabolites. However, there is limited information about the diversity of endophytic microbial communities, including bacteria, fungi, archaea, and viruses. Therefore, the study employs shotgun metagenomics to generate and make publicly available a dataset representing the leaf endophytic microbiome of Salvia officinalis.

**Data description:** Metagenomic DNA was extracted from leaves of S. officinalis collected as three biological replicates and sequenced using the Illumina NovaSeq X platform. Host-derived and contaminant sequences were removed by mapping reads to the S. officinalis reference genome using BWA-MEM. The resulting high-quality FASTQ files were analyzed to characterize the taxonomic composition of the endophytic microbiome using Kraken2-based classification.

**Keywords:** Salvia officinalis; Endophytic microbiome; Plant-microbe interactions; Shotgun metagenome sequencing; Taxonomic profiling.
