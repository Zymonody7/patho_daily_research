## 摘要
背景：快速准确地识别病原体对于传染病的临床管理至关重要。虽然二代测序（NGS）已经改变了病原体检测，但三代测序（TGS）在实时分析和长读长能力方面具有优势。本研究全面比较了 NGS 和 TGS 在多种临床样本中的诊断性能。方法：我们进行了一项前瞻性诊断准确性研究，涉及 105 份来自疑似感染患者的临床样本（58 份支气管肺泡灌洗液、28 份全血、19 份其他体液）。所有样本均采用 NGS（华大基因平台）和 TGS（纳米孔平台）进行分析。诊断性能根据结合了临床诊断、微生物培养和实验室检查结果的综合参考标准进行评估。结果：与 TGS 相比，NGS 表现出显著更高的灵敏度（95.9% vs. 82.4%，p < 0.001），而 TGS 则表现出更优的特异性（87.1% vs. 64.5%，p = 0.012）。两个平台之间的总体一致性为 85.7%（Kappa = 0.702）。NGS 在病毒检测（32 次 vs. 8 次检测，p < 0.001）和真菌识别（28 次 vs. 18 次检测，p = 0.023）方面表现出显著优势，而两种技术在细菌检测能力上相当。NGS 的 ROC 曲线下面积为 0.92，TGS 为 0.85。TGS 的周转时间显著缩短（中位时间 8 小时 vs. 30 小时，p < 0.001）。结论：NGS 和 TGS 在临床病原体检测中表现出互补的优势。NGS 具有更高的灵敏度，并增强了对病毒和真菌病原体的检测，适用于全面的诊断评估。TGS 提供更快速的结果和更高的特异性，有利于对时间敏感的临床决策。结合使用或针对特定场景的方法可能会优化临床实践中的病原体检测。

## Abstract
BACKGROUND: The rapid and accurate identification of pathogens is crucial for clinical management of infectious diseases. While Next-generation sequencing (NGS) has transformed pathogen detection, Third-generation sequencing (TGS) offers advantages in real-time analysis and long-read capabilities. This study comprehensively compares the diagnostic performance of NGS and TGS across diverse clinical samples. METHODS: We conducted a prospective diagnostic accuracy study involving 105 clinical samples (58 bronchoalveolar lavage fluid, 28 whole blood, 19 other body fluids) from patients with suspected infections. All samples were analyzed using both NGS (BGI platform) and TGS (Nanopore platform). Diagnostic performance was evaluated against a composite reference standard incorporating clinical diagnosis, microbiological culture, and laboratory findings. RESULTS: NGS demonstrated significantly higher sensitivity compared to TGS (95.9% vs. 82.4%, p < 0.001), while TGS showed superior specificity (87.1% vs. 64.5%, p = 0.012). The overall agreement between platforms was 85.7% (Kappa = 0.702). NGS exhibited particular advantages in viral detection (32 vs. 8 detections, p < 0.001) and fungal identification (28 vs. 18 detections, p = 0.023), whereas both technologies showed comparable bacterial detection capabilities. The area under the ROC curve was 0.92 for NGS and 0.85 for TGS. Turnaround time was significantly shorter for TGS (median 8 h vs. 30 h, p < 0.001). CONCLUSION: NGS and TGS demonstrate complementary strengths in clinical pathogen detection. NGS offers superior sensitivity and enhanced detection of viral and fungal pathogens, making it suitable for comprehensive diagnostic evaluation. TGS provides rapid results with higher specificity, advantageous for time-critical clinical decisions. A combined or scenario-specific approach may optimize pathogen detection in clinical practice.
