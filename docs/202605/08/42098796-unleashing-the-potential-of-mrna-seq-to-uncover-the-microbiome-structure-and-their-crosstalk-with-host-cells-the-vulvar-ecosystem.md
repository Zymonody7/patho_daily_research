## 摘要
背景：为了在单个样本中同时描述宿主基因表达和微生物组组成，传统上通常采用平行的实验和计算工作流（mRNA 测序以及 16S rRNA 基因或宏基因组学）。外阴环境因其在健康和疾病中的作用而成为一个新兴的研究领域。外阴位于阴道和会阴的交界处，其微生物组显示出一种中间特征，受到这两个生态系统的共同影响。结果：在利用定量微生物标准品（模拟群落）和宏基因组分析验证了 poly(A) 富集的 mRNA 测序在重建微生物群组成方面的可靠性后，我们分析了 30 例健康外阴样本的完整队列。至关重要的是，对整个队列的分析仅依赖于 mRNA 测序，而无需使用平行的 DNA 宏基因组学。这种统一的方法使我们不仅能够分析外阴细胞转录组，还能分析微生物群落的组成和动态，包括微生物基因表达特征。在同一标本上进行的这种三级分析（宿主 mRNA、单个细菌物种、细菌基因通路）进一步实现了宿主-微生物分子互作的基因级探索。利用这一统一框架，我们揭示了外阴微生物群显著的异质性和高度的个体间差异，并鉴定了与阴道描述类似的群落状态类型。重要的是，我们展示了不同的微生物配置与特定的宿主转录程序相关：卷曲乳杆菌（Lactobacillus crispatus）与上皮分化和屏障完整性相关，而富含阴道加德纳菌（Gardnerella vaginalis）或其他与菌群失调相关分类群的群落则表现出与炎症相关的转录特征。有趣的是，与较低保护作用相关的加氏乳杆菌（Lactobacillus gasseri）对外阴细胞表现出中间效应。结论：除了为这一研究不足的解剖部位提供新的生物学见解外，我们的研究还引入了一种具有重大领域影响的广泛适用策略。由于公共数据库中已有数万个现成的人类 RNA-seq 数据集，我们的方法能够从现有的转录组数据中回顾性地提取微生物组信息和宿主-微生物相互作用信号，而无需额外的测序或专门的微生物组方案。这为重新审视跨组织、疾病和低生物量环境的存档 RNA-seq 研究提供了一个强大且具有成本效益的机会，揭示了以前无法触及的宿主-微生物组互作层级，并最大化了已发表数据的科学价值。视频摘要。

## Abstract
BACKGROUND: To describe both host gene expression and microbiome composition in a single sample, parallel experimental and computational workflows (mRNA-sequencing and either 16S rRNA gene or metagenomics) have been traditionally applied. The vulvar milieu represents an area of emerging research for its role in health and disease. Located at the interface between the vagina and the perineum, the vulvar microbiome displays an intermediate signature, with influx from both ecosystems. RESULTS: Following validation of the reliability of poly(A)-enriched mRNA-sequencing in reconstructing the microbiota composition using both a quantitative microbial standard (mock) and metagenomic analysis, we analyze a full cohort of 30 healthy vulvar samples. Crucially, the analysis of the entire cohort relies solely on mRNA-sequencing without the use of parallel DNA metagenomics. This unified approach allows us to analyze not only the vulvar cell transcriptome, but also the composition and dynamics of microbial communities, including the microbial gene expression signatures. This three-level analysis (host-mRNA, individual bacterial species, bacterial gene pathways) on the very same specimens further enables a gene-level exploration of host-microbe molecular crosstalk. Using this unified framework, we reveal marked heterogeneity and high inter-individual variability in the vulvar microbiota, identifying community state types that mirror those described in the vagina. Importantly, we show that distinct microbial configurations are associated with specific host transcriptional programs: Lactobacillus crispatus correlates with epithelial differentiation and barrier integrity, whereas communities enriched in Gardnerella vaginalis, or other taxa associated with dysbiosis, exhibit transcriptional signatures linked to inflammation. Interestingly, Lactobacillus gasseri, which has been associated with lower protection, shows an intermediate effect on vulvar cells. CONCLUSIONS: Beyond providing new biological insights into an understudied anatomical niche, our study introduces a broadly applicable strategy with substantial impact for the field. With tens of thousands of human RNA-seq datasets already available in public repositories, our approach enables retrospective extraction of microbiome information and host-microbe interaction signals from existing transcriptomic data, without the need for additional sequencing or specialized microbiome protocols. This unlocks a powerful and cost-effective opportunity to revisit archived RNA-seq studies across tissues, diseases, and low-biomass environments, revealing previously inaccessible layers of host-microbiome crosstalk and maximizing the scientific value of published data.
