## 摘要
背景：尽管有数据表明撒哈拉以南非洲地区儿童中对第三代头孢菌素耐药的革兰氏阴性肠杆菌（GNE）流行率很高，但关于法属西非这些病原体耐药性的纵向描述仍然很少。方法：我们对2005年至2023年间在马里巴马科Gabriel Touré医院进行的一项侵袭性细菌感染研究中纳入的0-15岁病原体阳性儿童进行了回顾性分析。旨在描述病原体负担和非沙门氏菌GNE耐药性随时间的变化，并比较携带与不携带非沙门氏菌GNE病原体患者的院内死亡率。对2021年至2023年的分离株进行了全基因组测序，以鉴定赋予抗菌药物耐药性的基因。结果：在3803名病原体阳性患者中，392名培养出至少1种非沙门氏菌GNE病原体。2005-2023年间，病原体阳性患者中非沙门氏菌GNE的比例有所增加（从6%增至38%）。非沙门氏菌GNE对第三代头孢菌素的耐药率和多重耐药率分别从2005-2009年的30%和55%上升到2021-2023年的93%。与携带其他病原体的类似患者相比，来自巴马科以外的0-2个月患儿，以及来自巴马科市内外的3个月-15岁非沙门氏菌GNE患儿的死亡风险更高（比值比分别为3.17, 95% CI 1.69-5.95；2.13, 95% CI 1.44-3.14；以及2.25, 95% CI 1.38-3.66）。测序确认了新兴病原体弥散泛菌（Pantoea dispersa）的存在，并揭示了赋予多重耐药性的基因。结论：来自马里这家大型儿科转诊中心的数据显示，多重耐药革兰氏阴性肠杆菌的负担很高且在不断上升。这些模式反映了撒哈拉以南非洲地区日益增多的相关报告，突显了在类似环境中加强抗菌药物获取、诊断和管理策略的紧迫性。

## Abstract
BACKGROUND: Despite data demonstrating the high prevalence of third-generation cephalosporin resistant Gram-negative Enterobacterales (GNE) among children in sub-Saharan Africa, longitudinal descriptions of resistance among these pathogens from Francophone West Africa remain rare. METHODS: We conducted a retrospective analysis of pathogen-positive children 0-15 years old included in an invasive bacterial infection study at l'Hôpital Gabriel Touré (in Bamako, Mali) from 2005 to 2023. We aimed to describe changes in pathogen burden and non-Salmonella GNE resistance over time and compare in-hospital mortality between patients with and without non-Salmonella GNE pathogens. Isolates from 2021 to 2023 underwent whole-genome sequencing to identify genes conferring antimicrobial resistance. RESULTS: Of 3803 pathogen-positive patients, 392 grew at least 1 non-Salmonella GNE pathogen. The proportion of pathogen-positive patients with non-Salmonella GNE increased (6% vs 38%) from 2005-2023. Third-generation cephalosporin and multidrug resistance among non-Salmonella GNE increased from 30% and 55%, respectively, in 2005-2009, to 93% (both) by 2021-2023. Children 0-2 months old from outside Bamako and 3 months-15 years old from and from outside Bamako with non-Salmonella GNE had higher mortality odds (3.17, 95% CI 1.69-5.95; 2.13, 95% CI 1.44-3.14; and 2.25, 95% CI 1.38-3.66, respectively) than similar patients with other pathogens. Sequencing confirmed the presence of the emerging pathogen Pantoea dispersa and revealed genes conferring multidrug resistance. CONCLUSIONS: Data from this large pediatric referral center in Mali show a high and rising burden of multidrug-resistant Gram-negative Enterobacterales. These patterns reflect concerns increasingly reported across sub-Saharan Africa, highlighting the urgency of strengthening antimicrobial access, diagnostics, and stewardship strategies in similar settings.
