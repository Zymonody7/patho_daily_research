## 摘要
皮肤微生物组由细菌、真菌、病毒和古菌组成，通过调节免疫反应、维持屏障功能和影响代谢过程，在维持皮肤健康中发挥着至关重要的作用。微生物组失调与特应性皮炎 (AD)、银屑病和痤疮等皮肤病密切相关，特定的微生物（如金黄色葡萄球菌 Staphylococcus aureus 和痤疮切割杆菌 Cutibacterium acnes）在其中起到促进或预防疾病的作用。本综述总结了在理解皮肤微生物组的组成与功能、其与宿主免疫的相互作用以及对皮肤健康影响方面的最新进展。我们还讨论了环境和生物因素对微生物组稳定性的影响，并探讨了针对皮肤微生物群的新兴治疗策略。这些见解强调了基于微生物组的方法在推动皮肤病治疗方面的潜力。

## Abstract
SUMMARYThe skin microbiome, consisting of bacteria, fungi, viruses, and archaea, plays a crucial role in maintaining skin health by regulating immune responses, preserving barrier function, and influencing metabolic processes. Dysbiosis in the microbiome is linked to dermatological diseases such as atopic dermatitis (AD), psoriasis, and acne, with specific microbes, such as Staphylococcus aureus and Cutibacterium acnes, either promoting or protecting against disease. This review summarizes recent advances in understanding the composition and functions of the skin microbiome, its interaction with host immunity, and impact on skin health. We also discuss the implications of environmental and biological factors on microbiome stability and explore emerging therapeutic strategies targeting the skin microbiota. These insights highlight the potential of microbiome-based approaches for advancing dermatological treatment.
