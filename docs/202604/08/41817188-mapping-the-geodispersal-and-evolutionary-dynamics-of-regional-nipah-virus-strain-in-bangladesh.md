## 摘要
尼帕病毒（NiV）是一种人畜共患病原体，可引起人类严重的呼吸道和神经系统疾病，致死率极高，主要通过受蝙蝠污染的生椰枣汁和水果从果蝠传播给人类。孟加拉国 64 个地区中的 34 个地区几乎每年都会发生 NiV 疫情。本研究调查了 NiV 的地理扩散模式和演化系统动力学，旨在为孟加拉国制定更有效的疫情管理策略提供支持。研究利用 2016 年至 2023 年间从人类和蝙蝠样本中分离出的核蛋白（N）基因序列（n = 23）以及截至 2024 年 11 月的公开 NiV 基因组，进行了系统动力学和地理演化分析。对 NiV 时空数据的贝叶斯演化分析表明，NiV 进化支正在扩张并出现了一个新谱系，这可能是由随时间推移的遗传突变驱动的，并确定了 NiV 感染的潜在风险区域。分析显示，孟加拉国的 NiV 毒株在 1984 年左右分化为两个主要谱系：BD-1 和 BD-2。BD-1 分为 NiV-BD1.1 和 BD1.2（2004 年首次检测到），而 BD-2 出现于 2008 年，随后分化为 BD2.1 和 BD2.2。截至 2023 年，大多数源自蝙蝠的 NiV 序列属于 BD-2 谱系。孟加拉国 NiV 的估计演化速率为 4.89 × 10⁻⁴ 替换/位点/年（95% HPD：3.71 × 10⁻⁴ - 6.45 × 10⁻⁴），遗传簇跨度从 6.54 公里到 116.09 公里不等。我们的研究结果强调了孟加拉国 NiV 的持续演化、亚谱系的出现以及地理扩张，突显了系统发育地理学方法在指导针对性监测和控制策略方面的价值。重要性：全面了解尼帕病毒的传播和演化至关重要，因为它在孟加拉国引起严重的疾病和频繁的疫情。本研究详细探讨了近二十年来不同尼帕病毒株的地理迁移和遗传变化。通过分析来自果蝠和感染病例的病毒样本，研究揭示该病毒已形成两个截然不同的亚谱系，在孟加拉国的不同时间和地区独立演化。识别这些群体有助于确定未来疫情风险最高的区域。这些见解对于识别高风险地区并为早期检测和准备工作提供监测重点具有重要意义，特别是在发生反复溢出事件的地区。最终，这项研究强调了持续监测蝙蝠和人类中尼帕病毒演化的重要性，为管理和减轻这种危险疾病在孟加拉国及其他地区的影响提供了公共卫生策略指导。

## Abstract
Nipah virus (NiV) is a zoonotic pathogen causing severe respiratory and neurological disease in humans, with high fatality rates, primarily transmitted from fruit bats to humans through bat-tainted raw date palm sap and fruits. NiV outbreaks have occurred almost annually across 34 of 64 districts in Bangladesh. The study investigated the geodispersal patterns and evolutionary phylodynamics of NiV, aiding in more effective outbreak management strategies in Bangladesh. Phylodynamic and geographic evolutionary analyses were conducted using the nucleoprotein (N) gene sequence (n = 23) isolated from human and bat samples (2016-2023), along with publicly available NiV genomes up through November 2024. Bayesian evolutionary analysis of NiV spatiotemporal data suggested NiV clade expansion and the emergence of a new lineage, possibly driven by genetic mutations over time, and identified potential risk areas for NiV infections. The analysis revealed that Bangladeshi NiV strains diverged into two major lineages, BD-1 and BD-2, around 1984. BD-1 is split into NiV-BD1.1 and BD1.2 (first detected in 2004), while BD-2 emerged in 2008, later diverging into BD2.1 and BD2.2. As of 2023, most bat-derived NiV sequences belong to the BD-2 lineage. The estimated evolutionary rate of NiV in Bangladesh is 4.89 × 10⁻⁴ substitutions/site/year (95% HPD: 3.71 × 10⁻⁴ - 6.45 × 10⁻⁴), with genetic clusters spanning from 6.54 km to 116.09 km. Our findings highlight ongoing evolution, the emergence of sub-lineages, and the geographic expansion of NiV in Bangladesh, underscoring the value of phylogeographic approaches in guiding targeted surveillance and control strategies.IMPORTANCEA comprehensive understanding of the spread and evolution of the Nipah virus is critical, as it causes severe disease and frequent outbreaks in Bangladesh. This study provides a detailed look into how different strains of the Nipah virus have geographically moved and genetically changed over nearly two decades. By analyzing virus samples from fruit bats and infected human cases, the study reveals that the virus has formed two distinct sub-lineages, evolving separately at different times and regions of Bangladesh. Identifying these groups helps pinpoint areas most at risk for future outbreaks. These insights are important for identifying high-risk regions and informing surveillance priorities for early detection and preparedness, particularly in areas with recurring spillover events. Ultimately, this research emphasizes the importance of continuously monitoring Nipah virus evolution in both bats and humans, guiding public health strategies to manage and reduce the impact of this dangerous disease in Bangladesh and other regions.
