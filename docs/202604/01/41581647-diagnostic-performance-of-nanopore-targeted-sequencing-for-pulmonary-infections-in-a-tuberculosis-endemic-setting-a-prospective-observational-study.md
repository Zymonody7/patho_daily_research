## 摘要
背景：结核病（TB）流行地区的肺部感染具有异质性，且通常为多种微生物感染。纳米孔靶向测序（NTS）能够在单次靶向检测中同时检测分枝杆菌、细菌和真菌。然而，在结核病流行人群中，NTS 针对不同病原体类别的表现研究仍然有限。方法：我们在一家结核病专科医院开展了一项前瞻性研究，纳入了疑似肺部感染的成年患者，涵盖五个预定义的诊断类别：肺结核、非结核分枝杆菌肺病、细菌性感染、真菌性感染和多种微生物感染。对来自 305 名患者的呼吸道标本（n = 312）同时进行了常规微生物检测（CMT）和 NTS。以盲法临床诊断作为参考标准。结果：在 283 例配对病例中，NTS 识别出 263 例判定的病原体，而培养法识别出 185 例。NTS 对结核分枝杆菌的敏感性/特异性分别为 83.0%/99.4%，对非结核分枝杆菌为 89.8%/98.2%，对真菌为 92.9%/91.1%，对细菌为 97.4%/57.8%。在 72 例多种微生物感染中，NTS 检测到所有判定病原体的比例为 77.8%，而 CMT 为 62.5%，差异无统计学意义（P = 0.06）。总体而言，NTS 使诊断阳性率提高了 12.2%。结论：NTS 为结核病流行地区的多种肺部病原体提供了灵敏的单次检测手段。通过优化工作流程并提高对难培养生物或共感染生物的检测能力，NTS 可作为常规方法的补充。然而，非无菌呼吸道标本中的细菌 NTS 结果仍需结合临床进行分析。

## Abstract
BACKGROUND: Pulmonary infections in tuberculosis (TB)-endemic settings are heterogeneous and commonly polymicrobial. Nanopore-targeted sequencing (NTS) enables detection of mycobacteria, bacteria, and fungi in a single targeted assay. However, performance across pathogen classes in TB-endemic cohorts remains limited. METHODS: We conducted a prospective study at a specialized TB hospital, enrolling adults with suspected pulmonary infections across five predefined diagnostic categories: pulmonary TB, nontuberculous mycobacterial pulmonary disease, bacterial, fungal, and polymicrobial infection. Respiratory specimens (n = 312) from 305 patients were tested in parallel by conventional microbiological testing (CMT) and NTS. Blinded clinical diagnoses served as the reference standard. RESULTS: Among 283 paired cases, NTS identified the adjudicated pathogens in 263 cases, whereas culture identified them in 185 cases. NTS showed a sensitivity/specificity of 83.0%/99.4% for Mycobacterium tuberculosis, 89.8%/98.2% for nontuberculous mycobacteria, 92.9%/91.1% for fungi, and 97.4%/57.8% for bacteria. In 72 polymicrobial infections, NTS detected all adjudicated pathogens in 77.8% vs 62.5% for CMT, a nonsignificant difference (P = 0.06). Overall, NTS provided a 12.2% incremental diagnostic yield. CONCLUSION: NTS offers sensitive, single-assay detection of diverse pulmonary pathogens in TB-endemic settings. By streamlining workflows and improving detection of fastidious or co-infecting organisms, it may complement conventional methods. However, bacterial NTS findings in nonsterile respiratory specimens require clinical correlation.
