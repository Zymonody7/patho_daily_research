## 摘要
尽管在抗菌靶点识别方面取得了重大进展，但将经过生化验证的靶点转化为临床有效疗法的过程仍不一致，尤其是在针对革兰氏阴性病原体时。参与细菌细胞包膜生物合成的酶（如 LpxC 和 NagA）就是这种脱节的典型代表。这两个靶点都经过了遗传学验证，结构上易于处理，并得到了广泛的表征，但目前尚无抑制剂进入临床使用。在本文中，我们认为这种差距反映了早期靶点评估的局限性，而非这些酶本身的内在缺陷。以 LpxC 和 NagA 为案例研究，我们强调了背景依赖的必需性、代谢适应性、通路稳健性、宿主微环境以及药代动力学约束如何共同影响转化结果。我们进一步讨论了系统级和计算方法，这些方法揭示了经典验证范式所忽视的适应性缓冲和补偿机制。最后，我们提出了一个基于可靠性的抗菌靶点优先级排序框架，该框架整合了生理不可或缺性、通路脆弱性、旁路潜力以及转化可行性。在药物发现早期引入可靠性驱动的标准，可能会改善靶点选择并减少抗菌药物开发后期的损耗。

## Abstract
Despite major advances in antibacterial target identification, the translation of biochemically validated targets into clinically effective therapies remains inconsistent, particularly for Gram-negative pathogens. Enzymes involved in bacterial cell envelope biogenesis, such as LpxC and NagA, exemplify this disconnect. Both targets are genetically validated, structurally tractable, and extensively characterized, yet no inhibitors have reached clinical use. Here, we argue that this gap reflects limitations in early target assessment rather than intrinsic flaws in these enzymes. Using LpxC and NagA as case studies, we highlight how context-dependent essentiality, metabolic adaptability, pathway robustness, host microenvironments, and pharmacokinetic constraints collectively shape translational outcomes. We further discuss systems-level and computational approaches that expose adaptive buffering and compensatory mechanisms overlooked by classical validation paradigms. Finally, we propose a reliability-based framework for antibacterial target prioritization that integrates physiological indispensability, pathway fragility, bypass potential, and translational feasibility. Incorporating reliability-driven criteria early in discovery may improve target selection and reduce late-stage attrition in antibacterial drug development.
