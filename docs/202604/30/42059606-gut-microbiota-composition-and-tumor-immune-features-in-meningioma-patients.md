## 摘要
未标注：脑膜瘤是常见的颅内肿瘤，其神经外驱动因素尚不明确。虽然肠-脑轴影响神经肿瘤的发生，但脑膜瘤特异性的肠道微生物群改变及其临床意义仍未得到表征。本研究整合了15名初治WHO I级脑膜瘤患者（MPs）和15名健康对照者（HCs）粪便样本的16S rRNA测序，以及肿瘤免疫浸润（MPO⁺中性粒细胞、CD68⁺巨噬细胞、CD3⁺ T细胞）的免疫组化分析。与HCs相比，MPs的α多样性显著降低（Shannon指数，P = 0.026），且β多样性存在显著差异（置换多因素方差分析，P < 0.0009）。分类学分析显示变形菌门（Proteobacteria）富集（28.82% vs. 2.46%，P = 0.001），特别是属水平上的埃希氏菌-志贺氏菌属（Escherichia_Shigella）（25.95% vs. 1.61%，P = 0.008），同时拟杆菌科（Bacteroidaceae）和瘤胃菌科（Ruminococcaceae）减少。LEfSe分析确定埃希氏菌-志贺氏菌属为脑膜瘤富集程度最高的生物标志物。在诊断模型中，埃希氏菌-志贺氏菌属检测WHO I级脑膜瘤的受试者工作特征曲线下面积（AUC）达到95.11%（95% CI: 86.91%-100%）。关键的是，埃希氏菌-志贺氏菌属的丰度与瘤内MPO⁺、CD68⁺和CD3⁺细胞密度呈正相关（均P < 0.05），而瘤胃菌科则呈负相关。16S rRNA测序数据已公开于GSA数据库，登录号为CRA027974。本研究首次提供了I级脑膜瘤肠道菌群失调的证据，其特征为埃希氏菌-志贺氏菌属占主导地位且免疫调节共生菌减少。这一特征与免疫浸润增加相关，并有望成为一种新型生物标志物。重要性：深入探索宿主-微生物相互作用具有重要价值。肠道菌群失调与包括胶质瘤在内的多种癌症类型的神经炎症、血脑屏障破坏和肿瘤发生有关。然而，此前尚未有关于脑膜瘤患者肠道微生物群组成和代谢特征的报道。为了填补这些关键知识空白，我们开展了一项整合16S rRNA测序、临床表型分析和免疫组化分析的病例对照研究。我们的研究揭示了脑膜瘤患者肠道微生物群的显著改变，其特征是α多样性降低、变形菌门富集以及包括拟杆菌科和瘤胃菌科在内的有益分类群减少。关键的是，我们将埃希氏菌-志贺氏菌属确定为潜在的诊断生物标志物，并证明了埃希氏菌-志贺氏菌属/肠杆菌科丰度的升高与瘤内MPO⁺中性粒细胞、CD68⁺巨噬细胞和CD3⁺ T细胞浸润增加之间存在强相关性。这些发现是肠道微生物群失调与脑膜瘤炎症密切相关的首个证据。

## Abstract
UNLABELLED: Meningiomas are prevalent intracranial tumors with poorly understood extraneural drivers. While the gut-brain axis influences neuro-oncogenesis, meningioma-specific gut microbiome alterations and their clinical implications remain uncharacterized. This study integrated 16S rRNA sequencing of fecal samples from 15 treatment-naïve WHO grade I meningioma patients (MPs) and 15 healthy controls (HCs) with immunohistochemical profiling of tumor immune infiltrates (MPO⁺ neutrophils, CD68⁺ macrophages, CD3⁺ T cells). Compared with HCs, MPs exhibited significantly reduced alpha diversity (Shannon index, P = 0.026) and distinct beta diversity (permutational multivariate analysis of variance, P < 0.0009). Taxonomic analysis revealed enrichment of Proteobacteria (28.82% vs. 2.46%, P = 0.001), specifically Escherichia_Shigella at the genus level (25.95% vs. 1.61%, P = 0.008), along with depletion of Bacteroidaceae and Ruminococcaceae. LEfSe identified Escherichia_Shigella as the top meningioma-enriched biomarker. In diagnostic modeling, Escherichia_Shigella achieved an area under the receiver operating characteristic curve of 95.11% (95% CI: 86.91%-100%) for WHO grade I meningioma detection. Critically, Escherichia_Shigella abundance positively correlated with intratumoral MPO⁺, CD68⁺, and CD3⁺ cell densities (all P < 0.05), whereas Ruminococcaceae showed inverse correlations. The 16S rRNA sequencing data are publicly available in the GSA database under accession number CRA027974. This study provides the first evidence of gut dysbiosis in grade I meningioma, characterized by Escherichia_Shigella dominance and depletion of immunomodulatory commensals. This signature correlates with increased immune infiltration and holds promise as a novel biomarker. IMPORTANCE: Detailed exploration of host-microbe interactions can be worthwhile. Gut dysbiosis has been implicated in neuroinflammation, blood-brain barrier disruption, and oncogenesis in multiple cancer types, including gliomas. However, the gut microbiota composition and metabolic characteristics in patients with meningioma have not been previously reported. To address these critical knowledge gaps, we conducted a case-control study integrating 16S rRNA sequencing, clinical phenotyping, and immunohistochemical profiling. Our study revealed significant alterations in the gut microbiota of MPs, characterized by reduced alpha diversity, enrichment of Proteobacteria, and depletion of beneficial taxa, including Bacteroidaceae and Ruminococcaceae. Critically, we identified Escherichia_Shigella as a potential diagnostic biomarker and demonstrated strong correlations between elevated Escherichia_Shigella/Enterobacteriaceae abundance and increased intratumoral infiltration of MPO⁺ neutrophils, CD68⁺ macrophages, and CD3⁺ T cells. These findings are the first evidence that gut microbiome dysbiosis is closely associated with meningioma inflammation.
