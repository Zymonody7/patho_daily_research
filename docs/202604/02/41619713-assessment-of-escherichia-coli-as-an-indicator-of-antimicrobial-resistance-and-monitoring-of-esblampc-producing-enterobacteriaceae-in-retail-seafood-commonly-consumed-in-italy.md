## 摘要
本研究在“全健康”（One Health）视角下，结合微生物学和基因组分析，评估了大肠杆菌（Escherichia coli）作为零售海鲜中抗菌素耐药性（AMR）指标的作用。总体而言，从意大利常用的七类海鲜的 422 份原始样本中，共培养出 55 份（13%）大肠杆菌。在多种海鲜类型中均检测到大肠杆菌，尤其是在双壳类（23%）和甲壳类（20%）中。由于这些物种可以从水环境或收获后的链条中积累或获取粪便污染物，因此它们是 AMR 监测的相关基质。与此同时，我们调查了产超广谱 β-内酰胺酶（ESBL）、AmpC 和碳青霉烯酶的肠杆菌科细菌的存在情况。在双壳类中以较低但具有相关性的频率鉴定出耐头孢噻肟的大肠杆菌分离株，并对其进行了表型和全基因组测序鉴定。大多数分离株携带具有临床重要性的 ESBL 或 AmpC 基因，其中 blaCTX-M-15 最为常见，且通常位于预测为质粒相关的重叠群（contigs）上，表明其具有水平传播的潜力。此外，还从冷冻虾中回收了一株携带 blaCTX-M-55 的产 ESBL 的赫氏大西洋杆菌（Atlantibacter hermannii）。在部分样本中还检测到了其他产 AmpC 的肠杆菌科细菌，包括柠檬酸杆菌属（Citrobacter）和肠杆菌属（Enterobacter）。总体而言，我们的研究结果支持大肠杆菌作为零售双壳类和甲壳类海鲜 AMR 指标生物的适用性。检测到携带重要质粒介导耐药决定因素的产 ESBL 菌株，凸显了加强这些海鲜商品中 AMR 监测的必要性。

## Abstract
This study assessed Escherichia coli as an indicator of antimicrobial resistance (AMR) in retail seafood, integrating microbiological and genomic analyses within a One Health perspective. Overall, E. coli was cultured from 55 (13%) of 422 raw samples from seven seafood categories commonly consumed in Italy. E. coli was detected across multiple seafood types, but particularly in bivalves (23%) and crustaceans (20%). Because these species can accumulate or acquire faecal contaminants from the aquatic environment or along the post-harvest chain, they represent relevant matrices for AMR monitoring. In parallel, we investigated the presence of Extended-β-Lactamase ESBL-, AmpC- and carbapenemase-producing Enterobacteriaceae. Cefotaxime-resistant E. coli isolates were identified at low but relevant frequencies, especially in bivalves, and were characterised phenotypically and by whole-genome sequencing. Most isolates carried clinically important ESBL or AmpC genes, more frequently blaCTX-M-15, often on contigs predicted as plasmid-associated, indicating their potential for horizontal spread. An ESBL-producing Atlantibacter hermannii harbouring blaCTX-M-55 was also recovered from frozen shrimps. Additional AmpC-producing Enterobacteriaceae, including Citrobacter and Enterobacter species, were detected in a subset of samples. Overall, our findings support the suitability of E. coli as an AMR indicator organism for retail bivalves and crustaceans. The detection of ESBL-producing strains, carrying important plasmid-borne resistance determinants, highlights the need to strengthen AMR surveillance in these seafood commodities.
