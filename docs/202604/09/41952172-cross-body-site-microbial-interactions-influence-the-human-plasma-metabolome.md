## 摘要
背景：人类微生物组深刻影响宿主血浆代谢组和健康，但大多数研究仅孤立地关注肠道微生物组。目前尚缺乏对多个身体部位微生物组如何共同塑造宿主代谢的全面评估。结果：利用来自三个独立人类队列的数据（n = 435），我们系统地分析了多个身体部位微生物组对人类血浆代谢组（814个已注释代谢物）的选择性和共同影响。所有身体部位的微生物组都对血浆代谢组的变异有所贡献，共同解释了30.13%的个体间差异。肠道微生物组的贡献最大（18.44%），其次是口腔（14.70%）、皮肤（11.5%）和鼻腔（5.88%）微生物组。微生物组成并不一定能预测代谢功能，例如，尽管组成截然不同，口腔和肠道微生物组与循环代谢物表现出相似的关联。机器学习和中介分析揭示了跨身体部位广泛存在的协作和协同微生物相互作用，特别是在口-肠轴上。超过一半的代谢物受到多个身体部位微生物组的共同影响。该轴表现出跨部位的微生物对话和顺序代谢处理，调节诸如吲哚衍生物和羧酸等代谢物。口-肠微生物组-代谢组轴在胰岛素抵抗（IR）中进一步增强，将增强的微生物协作与代谢失调联系起来。结论：我们的研究结果揭示了微生物组-代谢组关系的系统性和交互性，并强调需要整合空间分布的微生物生态系统，以充分理解宿主代谢调节和疾病机制。视频摘要。

## Abstract
BACKGROUND: The human microbiome profoundly influences the host plasma metabolome and health, but most studies have focused on the gut microbiome in isolation. A comprehensive assessment of how microbiomes from multiple body sites jointly shape host metabolism has been lacking. RESULTS: Using data from three independent human cohorts (n = 435), we systematically analyzed the selective and joint influences of microbiomes from multiple body sites on the human plasma metabolome (814 annotated metabolites). Microbiomes from all body sites contributed to plasma metabolome variation, collectively explaining 30.13% of the inter-individual variation. The gut microbiome showed the largest contribution (18.44%), followed by oral (14.70%), skin (11.5%), and nasal (5.88%) microbiomes. Microbial composition did not necessarily predict metabolic function for example, despite distinct compositions, oral and gut microbiomes exhibited similar associations with circulating metabolites. Machine learning and mediation analyses revealed widespread cooperative and synergistic microbial interactions across body sites, particularly along the oral-gut axis. Over half of the metabolites were jointly influenced by multiple body-site microbiomes. This axis showed cross-site microbial crosstalk and sequential metabolic processing, regulating metabolites such as indole derivatives and carboxylic acids. The oral-gut microbiome-metabolome axis was further amplified in insulin resistance (IR), linking enhanced microbial cooperation to metabolic dysregulation. CONCLUSION: Our findings reveal the systemic and interactive nature of microbiome-metabolome relationships and highlight the need to integrate spatially distributed microbial ecosystems to fully understand host metabolic regulation and disease mechanisms.
