## 摘要
社区获得性耐甲氧西林金黄色葡萄球菌（CA-MRSA）是导致菌血症的主要原因，但其在这一恶劣环境中取得成功的遗传基础仍不明确。在本研究中，我们利用转座子定向插入位点测序（TraDIS）技术，通过在新鲜、免疫功能正常的血液中进行全基因组筛选，绘制了 CA-MRSA 菌株 USA300 JE2 的适应性图谱。我们鉴定了 76 个适应性所需的基因，包括涉及呼吸和中心碳代谢、血红素解毒以及嘌呤从头合成的基因。作为对适应性基因的验证，竞争实验证实，单独破坏 purA、purB、fbp、hssR 或 aroA2 会显著降低细菌在血液中的适应性。相反，失活特定的调节因子（如 saeRS 二组分系统、替代 σ 因子 σB）以及黏附素（包括 fnbA 和 clfA）则赋予了竞争优势。这些发现提供了金黄色葡萄球菌在生理相关血液模型中适应性要求的全基因组图谱，为进一步研究细菌对血管内环境的适应提供了平台。重要性：了解金黄色葡萄球菌如何在人类血流中维持适应性，对于解释其作为侵袭性病原体的成功至关重要。本研究对使金黄色葡萄球菌在血液中保持竞争力的基因进行了全面的全基因组定义，揭示了适应这一挑战性环境的关键生理需求。通过鉴定功能受损会削弱适应性的遗传功能，我们的研究结果突出了在宿主施加的压力下维持适应性和竞争力的特定途径。本研究扩展了先前在其他感染生态位中进行的全基因组调查，强调了生理环境在塑造细菌适应性方面的重要性，并鉴定了金黄色葡萄球菌谱系间共享的保守交叉适应性决定因素。这些见解增进了我们目前对金黄色葡萄球菌如何适应血流的理解，并为未来关于葡萄球菌病理生理学的功能和比较研究奠定了基础。

## Abstract
Community-associated methicillin-resistant Staphylococcus aureus (CA-MRSA) is a leading cause of bacteremia, yet the genetic basis for its success in this hostile environment remains poorly defined. In this study, we employed transposon-directed insertion site sequencing (TraDIS) to map the fitness landscape of CA-MRSA strain USA300 JE2 through a genome-wide screening in fresh, immunocompetent human blood. We identified 76 genes required for fitness, including genes involved in respiratory and central carbon metabolisms, heme detoxification, and de novo purine biosynthesis. As validation of fitness genes, competition assays confirmed that individual disruption of purA, purB, fbp, hssR, or aroA2 significantly reduced bacterial fitness in blood. Conversely, inactivation of specific regulators, such as the saeRS two-component system, the alternative sigma factor σB, and adhesins, including fnbA and clfA, conferred a competitive advantage. These findings provide a genome-scale map of S. aureus fitness requirements in a physiologically relevant blood model, offering a platform for further investigation of bacterial adaptation to the intravascular environment.IMPORTANCEUnderstanding how Staphylococcus aureus maintains fitness in the human bloodstream is essential for explaining its success as an invasive pathogen. This study provides a comprehensive, genome-wide definition of the genes that enable S. aureus to remain competitive in blood, revealing the key physiological requirements for adaptation to this challenging environment. By identifying genetic functions whose disruption impairs fitness, our findings highlight the specific pathways that sustain adaptation and competitiveness under host-imposed stress. Extending previous genome-scale investigations conducted in other infection niches, this study emphasizes the importance of physiological context in shaping bacterial fitness and identifies conserved cross-fitness determinants shared among S. aureus lineages. These insights advance our current understanding of how S. aureus adapts to the bloodstream and strengthen the foundation for future functional and comparative studies on staphylococcal pathophysiology.
