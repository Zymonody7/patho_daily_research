## 摘要
病毒群落，尤其是噬菌体，影响原核生物的多样性，从而影响宿主的代谢过程。然而，鸡肠道病毒组的组成和作用仍知之甚少。为了填补这一空白，我们挖掘了 1,458 个鸡肠道宏基因组和 56 个病毒富集样本，以回收病毒序列并组装了一套全面的病毒基因组草图。我们鉴定了 19,778 个病毒操作分类单元 (vOTUs)，其中 97% 是来自有尾噬菌体纲 (Caudoviricetes) 的双链 DNA (dsDNA) 噬菌体，主要针对乳杆菌属 (Lactobacillus)、粘液乳杆菌属 (Limosilactobacillus) 和埃希氏菌属 (Escherichia) 等肠道细菌。这些基因组中的大多数蛋白质编码基因尚未被表征，且缺乏已知的生物学功能。此外，vOTUs 在样本中的分布表明，鸡病毒组具有高度的个体特异性。然而，病毒群落还表现出沿胃肠道的强烈空间分层，近端和远端区域之间存在显著差异，这主要由与乳杆菌科 (Lactobacillaceae) 相关的噬菌体驱动。此外，本研究表明地理区域、品种和饮食驱动了鸡肠道病毒的多样性和组成。这强调了鸡肠道病毒组显著的新颖性及其在很大程度上尚未探索的功能潜力，如果分析仅限于粪便样本，其中大部分将被遗漏。重要性：鸡肠道蕴藏着庞大的病毒群落，尽管它们具有影响家禽健康和生产力的潜力，但在很大程度上仍未被探索。通过分析来自 15 个国家不同肠道区域的 1,514 个样本，我们发现了近 20,000 种不同的病毒，其中大多数是以前未知的噬菌体。鸡病毒组在胃肠道中表现出强烈的空间差异，这意味着每个肠段都拥有独特的病毒群落，这强调了仅凭粪便样本会遗漏病毒组的大部分多样性。我们还发现地理区域、品种和饮食可以驱动鸡肠道病毒的多样性和组成。总的来说，我们的发现极大地扩展了我们对肠道病毒多样性和微生物组生态学的理解，为制定监测或操纵微生物组以改善家禽健康的策略提供了宝贵的基础。

## Abstract
UNLABELLED: Viral communities, especially phages, affect prokaryotic diversity and thus influence the host's metabolic processes. However, the makeup and role of the chicken gut virome remain poorly understood. To address this gap, we mined 1,458 chicken gut metagenomes and 56 viral-enriched samples to recover viral sequences and assemble a comprehensive collection of draft viral genomes. We identified 19,778 viral operational taxonomic units (vOTUs), of which 97% were dsDNA phages from the Caudoviricetes class, primarily targeting gut bacteria such as Lactobacillus, Limosilactobacillus, and Escherichia. Most protein-coding genes in these genomes were uncharacterized and lacked known biological functions. Additionally, the distribution of vOTUs across samples showed that the chicken virome is highly individual-specific. Yet, the viral community also exhibited strong spatial stratification along the gastrointestinal tract, with notable differences between proximal and distal regions, primarily driven by phages linked to the Lactobacillaceae family. Moreover, this study shows that the geographical region, breed, and diet drive the chicken gut viral diversity and composition. This underscores the significant novelty of the chicken gut virome and its largely unexplored functional potential, much of which would be missed if analyses were restricted to fecal samples. IMPORTANCE: The chicken gut harbors a vast community of viruses that remain largely unexplored despite their potential to influence poultry health and productivity. By analyzing 1,514 samples from different gut regions across 15 countries, we discovered nearly 20,000 distinct viruses, most of which were previously unknown phages. The chicken virome showed strong spatial differences along the gastrointestinal tract, meaning each gut section harbors a unique viral community, underscoring that fecal samples alone miss much of the virome's diversity. We also uncovered that the geographical region, breed, and diet could drive the chicken gut viral diversity and composition. Overall, our findings greatly expand our understanding of gut virus diversity and microbiome ecology, offering a valuable foundation for developing strategies to monitor or manipulate the microbiome to improve poultry health.
