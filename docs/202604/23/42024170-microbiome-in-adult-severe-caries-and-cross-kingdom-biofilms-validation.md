## 摘要
目的：成人重度龋病（ASC）是成年期发生的一种猖獗龋，会导致口腔功能严重受损并降低生活质量。然而，ASC 的致病机制尚不明确。本研究旨在鉴定 ASC 患者的核心微生物群，并初步探讨 ASC 相关关键核心微生物的相互作用及其致病性。材料与方法：收集 7 名成人重度龋病患者和 6 名无龋志愿者的唾液样本进行宏基因组分析。根据微生物组分析结果，建立了一个由变形链球菌（S. mutans）、白色念珠菌（C. albicans）和小韦荣氏菌（V. parvula）组成的体外跨界生物膜模型，以模拟高龋风险微环境。利用扫描电子显微镜（SEM）、结晶紫（CV）染色和细菌活死染色评估生物膜的形成。通过产酸实验、酸应激挑战实验、共聚焦激光扫描显微镜（CLSM）和 qRT-PCR 分析产酸能力和胞外多糖（EPS）的合成。此外，利用原子力显微镜（AFM）评估脱矿牙本质切片的表面粗糙度。结果：宏基因组分析显示，高龋易感患者唾液中白色念珠菌和小韦荣氏菌显著富集。与单一物种的变形链球菌生物膜相比，体外培养的跨界生物膜表现出更强的生长能力和 EPS 合成能力。此外，跨界生物膜显著增加了脱矿样本的表面粗糙度，其作用强于单物种和双物种生物膜。结论：白色念珠菌和小韦荣氏菌的定植增加了生物膜生物量，增强了微生物在应激下的存活能力，并提升了生物膜的毒力，从而在体外诱导牙本质切片脱矿。临床意义：本研究表明，ASC 患者中龋病相关微生物间的种间相互作用增强了其毒力和致龋性，为高龋易感性的研究和预防提供了新见解。

## Abstract
OBJECTIVES: Adult severe caries (ASC) is a form of rampant caries that develops in adulthood, causing severe impairment of oral function and reducing quality of life. However, the pathogenic mechanism of ASC remains unclear. This study aimed to identify the core microbiota in patients with ASC and preliminarily investigate the microbial interactions and pathogenicity of key ASC-associated core microorganisms. MATERIALS AND METHODS: Saliva samples were collected from 7 adult patients with severe caries and 6 caries-free volunteers for metagenomic analysis. Based on microbiome profiling results, an in vitro cross-kingdom biofilm model composed of Streptococcus mutans (S. mutans), Candida albicans (C. albicans) and Veillonella parvula (V. parvula) was established to simulate a high caries-risk microenvironment. Scanning electron microscopy (SEM), crystal violet (CV) staining, and live/dead bacterial staining were used to evaluate biofilm formation. Acid production assays, acid stress challenge tests, confocal laser scanning microscopy (CLSM) and qRT-PCR were performed to analyze the acidogenicity and synthesis of extracellular polysaccharides (EPS). Additionally, atomic force microscopy (AFM) was used to assess the surface roughness of demineralized dentin slices. RESULTS: Metagenomic analysis revealed significant enrichment of C. albicans and V. parvula in the saliva of patients with high caries susceptibility. The in vitro cultured cross-kingdom biofilms exhibited enhanced growth and EPS synthesis compared with single-species S. mutans biofilms. Moreover, cross-kingdom biofilms significantly increased surface roughness of demineralized samples, with a stronger effect than single- and dual-species biofilms. CONCLUSIONS: Colonization by C. albicans and V. parvula increases biofilm biomass, enhances microbial survival under stress, and elevates biofilm virulence, which induces demineralization of dentin slices in vitro. CLINICAL RELEVANCE: This study demonstrates that the interspecies interactions among caries-related microorganisms in ASC patients confer enhanced virulence and cariogenicity, providing novel insights for the investigation and prevention of high caries susceptibility.
