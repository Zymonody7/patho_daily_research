## 摘要
背景：早花遏蓝菜（Noccaea praecox）是一种原产于意大利半岛和西巴尔干地区的锌（Zn）、镉（Cd）和铅（Pb）超积累植物，自然分布于含金属土壤和非含金属土壤中。在本研究中，我们调查了土壤金属浓度和植物宿主对微生物功能性状的影响，特别是两个土壤部位（早花遏蓝菜的根部和根际）中的抗性组（即与金属耐受性和抗性相关的微生物功能）。为此，我们从每个含金属和非含金属样点各采集了四株植物，并利用宏基因组测序方法对配对的根部和根际样本的微生物功能进行了表征；由于生物量有限，每个样点获得三个根部样本和四个根际样本。结果：部位是总体微生物功能结构的主要驱动因素。相比之下，土壤金属浓度和根部部位显著塑造了微生物抗性组。与钴-锌-镉外排系统和铜转运P型ATP酶V相关的功能在含金属样点显著富集，其log2倍数变化分别为2.62和1.72。与锰/铁和钴/镍相关的转运蛋白受宿主塑造，且与土壤金属水平无关，这与宿主介导的微生物功能筛选一致。值得注意的是，与ZIP家族相关的几种锌转运蛋白相关微生物功能在根际中丰度更高，这可能支持了植物对锌的高需求。结论：总体而言，我们的结果表明环境条件和植物宿主在塑造微生物功能潜力方面发挥着交互作用，且宿主的影响有时比土壤金属含量更强。在锌超积累植物早花遏蓝菜的根际中，锌转运蛋白（Zrt-/Irt样蛋白）的富集表明了一种特定的微生物适应性，可能有助于锌的吸收。这些发现为支持早花遏蓝菜生活方式的植物-微生物相互作用的功能动态提供了新见解。

## Abstract
BACKGROUND: Noccaea praecox is a zinc (Zn), cadmium (Cd), and lead (Pb) hyperaccumulating plant native to the Italian peninsula and Western Balkans, where it occurs naturally in both metalliferous and non-metalliferous soils. In the present study, we investigated the effects of soil metal concentrations and the plant host on microbial functional traits, specifically the resistome (i.e., microbial functions associated with metal tolerance and resistance) in two soil compartments: the roots and rhizosphere of N. praecox. For this, we collected four plants from each metalliferous and non-metalliferous site and used a metagenomic sequencing approach to characterise microbial functions from paired root and rhizosphere samples, with three root samples per site obtained due to limited biomass, and four rhizosphere samples. RESULTS: The compartment was the primary driver of the general microbial functional structure. By contrast, the soil metal concentrations and root compartment significantly shaped the microbial resistome. Functions associated with the cobalt-zinc-cadmium efflux system and copper-transporting P-type ATPase V were significantly enriched at the metalliferous compared to the non-metalliferous site, with log2 fold change being 2.62 and 1.72, respectively. Transporters associated with manganese/iron and cobalt/nickel were shaped by the host, regardless of soil metal levels, consistent with host-mediated filtering of microbial functions. Notably, several Zn transporter-related microbial functions associated with the ZIP family were more abundant in the rhizosphere, potentially supporting the plant's high Zn demand. CONCLUSION: Overall, our results demonstrate that both environmental conditions and plant host play interactive roles in shaping the microbial functional potential, with the host sometimes exerting a stronger influence than soil metal content. The enrichment of Zn transporters (Zrt-/Irt-like proteins) in the rhizosphere of the Zn-hyperaccumulating N. praecox suggests a specific microbial adaptation that may facilitate Zn uptake. These findings provide new insight into the functional dynamics of plant-microbe interactions that support the N. praecox lifestyle.
