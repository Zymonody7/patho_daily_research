## 摘要
由禽致病性大肠杆菌（APEC）引起的大肠杆菌病导致全球家禽生产遭受重大经济损失。多重耐药（MDR）APEC 的出现通过抗菌素耐药性（AMR）基因的水平转移构成了人畜共患风险。噬菌体疗法作为抗生素疗法的一种安全替代方案脱颖而出；然而，针对来自不同地理区域的 MDR-APEC 噬菌体的全面表征仍然有限。我们从印度五个邦采集的家禽粪便样本中分离出五种裂解性噬菌体，并通过形态分析、生理稳定性测试、全基因组测序和体内功效评估对其进行了表征。针对 APEC 分离株确定了宿主范围，并在大蜡螟（Galleria mellonella）感染模型中验证了治疗潜力。所有噬菌体均表现出肌尾噬菌体科（Myovirus）样形态，并在生理相关温度（高达 55°C-70°C）和 pH 条件（3-11）下保持稳定。这些噬菌体被分类为大肠杆菌噬菌体 vB_EcoM_fRPOT1、vB_EcoM_fDMYT1、vB_EcoM_fBSZT1、vB_EcoM_fUAMT1 和 vB_EcoM_fPKPT2。它们的基因组大小在 170 到 356 kb 之间，属于三个不同的属：Dhakavirus、Gaprivervirus 和 Asteriusvirus。基因组分析确认不存在抗菌素耐药性、毒力、毒素或溶源性基因。分离出 51 株 APEC 菌株，其中 23 株（45.1%）为多重耐药株。单个噬菌体裂解了 37%-51% 的测试 APEC 菌株和 17%-39% 的 MDR 菌株。与未处理对照组相比，三种噬菌体（fBSZT1、fUAMT1 和 fPKPT2）在感染复数（MOI）为 10 的大蜡螟感染模型中，显著将幼虫存活率提高至 60%-80%。本研究建立了一个针对 MDR-APEC 菌株且表征良好的噬菌体库，为开发基于噬菌体的干预措施奠定了基础，旨在减少抗生素依赖并在“全健康”（One Health）框架下降低 AMR 传播风险。重要性：家禽养殖中抗生素的过度使用已引发危机。多重耐药（MDR）细菌通过食物链威胁动物健康和人类安全。当抗生素失效时，养殖户面临毁灭性损失，耐药细菌可通过食用或环境污染传播给人类。噬菌体提供了一种切实可行的解决方案，因为它们在杀死目标细菌的同时不会伤害有益微生物或留下化学残留。我们的全面表征证实，这五种噬菌体是安全有效的，因为它们缺乏任何耐药或毒素基因，并能挽救 60%-80% 的受感染幼虫。这代表了一个针对印度家禽特定耐药菌株的表征噬菌体库。通过提供一种经过验证的抗生素替代方案，这项工作支持了可持续食品生产，同时减少了抗菌素耐药性从农场向人类的传播。

## Abstract
UNLABELLED: Colibacillosis, caused by avian pathogenic Escherichia coli (APEC), results in substantial economic losses in global poultry production. The emergence of multidrug-resistant (MDR) APEC poses zoonotic risks through horizontal transfer of antimicrobial resistance (AMR) genes. Bacteriophage therapy emerges as a safe alternative to antibiotherapy; however, comprehensive characterization of phages targeting MDR-APEC from diverse geographical regions remains limited. We isolated five lytic bacteriophages from poultry fecal samples collected from five Indian states and characterized them through morphological analysis, physiological stability testing, whole-genome sequencing, and in vivo efficacy assessment. Host range was determined against APEC isolates, and therapeutic potential was validated in the Galleria mellonella infection model. All phages showed Myovirus-like morphology and stability across physiologically relevant temperatures (up to 55°C-70°C) and pH conditions (3-11). Phages were classified as Escherichia phage vB_EcoM_fRPOT1, vB_EcoM_fDMYT1, vB_EcoM_fBSZT1, vB_EcoM_fUAMT1, and vB_EcoM_fPKPT2. Their genome size ranges from 170 to 356 kb, belonging to three distinct genera: Dhakavirus, Gaprivervirus, and Asteriusvirus. Genomic analysis confirmed the absence of antimicrobial resistance, virulence, toxin, or lysogeny genes. Fifty-one APEC strains were isolated, of which 23 (45.1%) were MDR. Individual phages lysed 37%-51% of tested APEC and 17%-39% of MDR strains. Three phages (fBSZT1, fUAMT1, and fPKPT2) significantly improved larval survival to 60%-80% at an MOI of 10 in G. mellonella infection models compared to the untreated control. This study establishes a well-characterized phage bank targeting MDR-APEC strains, providing a foundation for developing phage-based interventions to reduce antibiotic dependency and mitigate AMR transmission risks under the One Health framework. IMPORTANCE: The overuse of antibiotics in poultry farming has created a crisis. The multidrug-resistant (MDR) bacteria threaten both animal health and human safety through the food chain. When antibiotics fail, farmers face devastating losses, and resistant bacteria can transfer to humans through consumption or environmental contamination. Bacteriophages offer a practical solution as they kill target bacteria without harming beneficial microbes or leaving chemical residues. Our comprehensive characterization confirms that these five phages are safe and effective as they lack any resistance or toxin genes and rescue 60%-80% of infected larvae. This represents a characterized phage bank targeting the specific resistant strains in Indian poultry. By providing a validated alternative to antibiotics, this work supports sustainable food production while reducing the spread of antimicrobial resistance from farms to humans.
