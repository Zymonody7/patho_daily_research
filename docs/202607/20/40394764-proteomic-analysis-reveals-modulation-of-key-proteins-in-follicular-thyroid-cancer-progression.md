## 摘要
背景：由于滤泡状甲状腺腺瘤（FTA）与滤泡状甲状腺癌（FTC，第二常见的甲状腺癌形式）表现出几乎相同的细胞形态，细胞病理学无法可靠地将两者区分开来。鉴于诊断和治疗方面的挑战，本研究旨在确定 FTC 的潜在机制。方法：利用平行反应监测质谱（PRM-MS）分析，我们从来自 18 个临床中心的 1085 份 FTC 和 FTA 组织样本的回顾性队列中，鉴定并定量了 94 个差异表达的候选蛋白。在这些目标蛋白中，利用机器学习对具有区分 FTC 和 FTA 潜力的蛋白进行了优先级排序。通过共免疫沉淀（co-IP）和免疫荧光共定位实验，以及基因干扰、过表达和免疫组织化学（IHC）实验，研究了所选蛋白的相互作用和细胞功能。结果：利用机器学习模型和特征选择方法，94 个候选蛋白中的 30 个被确定为关键蛋白。使用 FTC 细胞系的 co-IP 和免疫荧光共定位实验揭示了胰岛素样生长因子 2 受体（IGF2R）、主要穹窿蛋白（MVP）、组蛋白去乙酰化酶 1（HDAC1）和组蛋白 H1.5（H1-5）之间的相互作用。在 FTC-133 细胞中进行的基因干扰和过表达实验证实了这些蛋白在细胞增殖中的促进作用。患者样本的 IHC 分析进一步证实，与 FTA 相比，这四种蛋白在 FTC 中的表达水平升高。结论：我们的研究结果强调了先进蛋白质组学技术在阐明 FTC 分子基础方面的效用，突出了 IGF2R、MVP、HDAC1 和 H1-5 在 FTC 进展中的潜在意义，并为探索靶向治疗奠定了基础。

## Abstract
BACKGROUND: Cytopathology cannot be used to reliably distinguish follicular thyroid adenoma (FTA) from follicular thyroid carcinoma (FTC), the second most common form of thyroid cancer, because they exhibit nearly identical cellular morphology. Given the challenges in diagnosis and treatment, this study aims to identify the mechanisms underlying FTC. METHODS: Using parallel reaction monitoring-mass spectrometry (PRM-MS) assays, we identified and quantified 94 differentially expressed protein candidates from a retrospective cohort of 1085 FTC and FTA tissue samples from 18 clinical centers. Of these targeted proteins, those with the potential for distinguishing FTC from FTA were prioritized using machine learning. Co-immunoprecipitation (co-IP) and immunofluorescence co-localization assays, as well as gene interference, overexpression, and immunohistochemistry (IHC) experiments, were used to investigate the interactions and cellular functions of selected proteins. RESULTS: Using machine learning models and feature selection methods, 30 of the 94 candidates were prioritized as key proteins. Co-IP and immunofluorescence co-localization assays using FTC cell lines revealed interactions among insulin-like growth factor 2 receptor (IGF2R), major vault protein (MVP), histone deacetylase 1 (HDAC1), and histone H1.5 (H1-5). Gene interference and overexpression experiments in FTC-133 cells confirmed the promotional role of these proteins in cell proliferation. IHC assays of patient samples further confirmed elevated expression of these four proteins in FTC compared with that in FTA. CONCLUSIONS: Our findings underscore the utility of advanced proteomic techniques in elucidating the molecular underpinnings of FTC, highlighting the potential significance of IGF2R, MVP, HDAC1, and H1-5 in FTC progression, and providing a foundation for the exploration of targeted therapies.
